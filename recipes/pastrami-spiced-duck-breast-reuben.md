## Ingredients

- 2  skin-on, boneless duck breast halves
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
- 2 teaspoons ground coriander
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- ½ teaspoon dry mustard
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper
- ½ cup olive oil
- ¼ cup sherry vinegar
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 cups chopped green cabbage
- 2 tablespoons Russian salad dressing, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon vegetable oil, or as needed
- 2 slices rye bread
- ¼ cup shredded Swiss cheese

## Instructions

1. Cut shallow slashes diagonally across the fat-side of each duck breast to create cross-hatch marks.
2. Whisk 1 tablespoon olive oil, 1 tablespoon black pepper, coriander, 1 teaspoon kosher salt, paprika, dry mustard, and cayenne pepper together in a large bowl until a wet-rub consistency.
3. Rub spice mixture onto all sides of duck breasts.
4. Set duck into bowl, cover the bowl with plastic wrap, and refrigerate, 8 hours to overnight.
5. Pat excess rub off of duck with paper towels.
6. Season on all sides with kosher salt.
7. Whisk 1/2 cup olive oil, sherry vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and black pepper together in a bowl until creamy and smooth.
8. Toss cabbage and Russian dressing together in a separate bowl.
9. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
10. Cook duck, skin-side down, in hot skillet until skin is crispy and caramelized, 5 to 6 minutes.
11. Reduce heat to medium, flip ducks breasts, and cook until pink in the middle and meat springs back when touched, about 5 minutes more.
12. Remove to a plate to rest.
13. Pour rendered duck fat from the skillet through a strainer into a bowl.
14. Heat 1 tablespoon reserved duck fat in a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
15. Place bread in skillet and cook until lightly toasted, about 1 minute.
16. Flip bread slices and cook until toasted on the other side, about 1 minute more.
17. Sprinkle cheese on each piece of bread, flip slices, and cook until cheese is melted and crisped, about 30 seconds.
18. Slice each duck breasts into 5 pieces.
19. Place each piece of toast on a plate and top with sliced duck breasts.
20. Drizzle Dijon mustard mixture over the top and serve with cabbage.
