## Ingredients

- 4 cups mussels
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons chopped garlic
- 2 tablespoons chopped shallots
- 2 cups beef broth, divided
- 2 tablespoons anise-flavored liqueur
- ½  jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced
- ½  red chile pepper, seeded and minced
- 1 cup white wine
- 10 leaves fresh basil, chopped
- ¼ cup light cream
- 2 teaspoons cornstarch
- 1  lemon, juiced

## Instructions

1. Soak mussels in a bowl of cold water for at least 10 minutes; remove from water and scrub with a stiff brush to remove dirt if needed.
2. Remove beard using a sharp knife if needed.
3. Melt butter with the olive oil in a large stockpot over medium heat; cook and stir garlic and shallots until lightly browned, 2 to 3 minutes.
4. Add 1 cup beef broth, anise-flavored liqueur, jalapeno pepper, and red chile pepper to garlic-shallot mixture; bring to a boil.
5. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until peppers are softened, about 5 minutes.
6. Stir wine, remaining 1 cup beef broth, and basil into broth mixture; simmer.
7. Whisk light cream and cornstarch together in a bowl until cornstarch is dissolved; add to broth mixture and bring to a boil.
8. Add mussels and lemon juice to boiling broth mixture and cover stockpot with a lid; cook until shells open, about 5 minutes.
9. Discard any shells that didn't open.
