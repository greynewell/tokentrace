## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons margarine
- ½ cup minced onion
- ½ cup minced green bell pepper
- 1 tablespoon dry mustard
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®)
- ¾ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon hot pepper sauce (such as Tabasco®)
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper
- ¼ cup all-purpose flour
- 1 ¼ cups light cream
- 1 pound crab meat
- 2  egg yolks, beaten
- ½ cup bread crumbs
- 1 tablespoon melted butter
- ½ teaspoon paprika
- 1 cup corn oil, or as needed

## Instructions

1. Melt margarine in a skillet over medium heat.
2. Cook and stir onion and green bell pepper in margarine until tender, about 5 minutes.
3. Stir mustard, Worcestershire sauce, seafood seasoning, salt, hot pepper sauce, black pepper, and cayenne pepper into the onion mixture.
4. Sprinkle flour over the mixture; stir to integrate.
5. Pour cream over the mixture; bring to a boil, stirring constantly and cook for 1 minute.
6. Fold crab meat into the mixture.
7. Spoon some of the liquid from the skillet into the bowl with the beaten egg yolks to temper the egg; stir.
8. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet and stir.
9. Cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens, about 5 minutes.
10. Transfer crab mixture to a bowl; refrigerate until chilled completely, about 1 hour.
11. Mix bread crumbs, melted butter, and paprika in a shallow bowl.
12. Divide the crab mixture into 4 portions; form into patties.
13. Gently press the patties into the bread crumb mixture to coat.
14. Pour enough corn oil into a flat-bottomed pan to be 1-inch deep; heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
15. Cook patties in hot oil until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side.
