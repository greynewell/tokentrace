## Ingredients

- 1 cup watercress, or as desired
- ½  lemon, zested and juiced
- 1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, softened
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon dried dill weed
- 1 teaspoon dried parsley
- 1 (17.5 ounce) package frozen puff pastry, thawed
- 2  boneless, skinless salmon fillets
- 1  egg, beaten

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
2. Place watercress in a sieve, pour boiling water over to wilt it, and drain on paper towels.
3. Combine wilted watercress with lemon zest in a small bowl.
4. Mix cream cheese, garlic, dill, and parsley together in a separate bowl.
5. Roll out 2 puff pastry sheets gently on a work surface until slightly larger than length of salmon fillets; cut sheets in half.
6. Place 1/2 watercress mixture in center of each sheet.
7. Spread 1/2 cream cheese mixture over watercress.
8. Center salmon fillets on top of cream cheese mixture; squeeze lemon juice over each.
9. Brush the edges of the pastry with some of the egg.
10. Fold top and bottom of pastry over the salmon; trim  edges to remove excess pastry.
11. Press pastry seams together with your fingers to seal.
12. Transfer wrapped fillets, seam-side down, to baking sheet.
13. Brush top with beaten egg.
14. Bake in preheated oven until pastry is golden brown, 20 to 25 minutes.
