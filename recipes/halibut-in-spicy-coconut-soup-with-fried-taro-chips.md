## Ingredients

- 1  taro, peeled and cut into large chunks
- ½ cup vegetable oil, or as needed, divided
- 4  bone-in halibut steaks
- 1 head green cabbage, quartered
- 4 small tomatoes, chopped
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 1  green chile pepper, finely chopped, or more to taste
- 2 (14 ounce) cans coconut milk
- 2 ½ cups water
- sea salt to taste
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
2. Add taro; cook until tender, 15 to 20 minutes.
3. Drain and let cool.
4. Cut cooled taro into 1/4-inch thick slices.
5. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
6. Cook halibut steaks in the hot oil until lightly browned, about 2 minutes per side.
7. Place halibut steaks on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
8. Place cabbage quarters in a large saucepan.
9. Pour in enough water to cover.
10. Bring to a boil; cook until just tender, 4 to 5 minutes.
11. Drain.
12. Spread cabbage evenly over the bottom of a large Dutch oven over medium heat.
13. Arrange halibut steaks on top.
14. Layer tomatoes, onion, and chile pepper over halibut.
15. Stir coconut milk and water together in a bowl.
16. Pour into the Dutch oven.
17. Season with salt.
18. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer until halibut flakes easily with a fork, about 30 minutes.
19. Avoid stirring too often to keep halibut intact.
20. Sprinkle cilantro on top.
21. Heat remaining 6 tablespoons vegetable oil in a deep saucepan over medium-high heat.
22. Cook taro slices in batches until slightly golden, 3 to 5 minutes.
23. Place on a wire rack to cool.
24. Season with sea salt.
