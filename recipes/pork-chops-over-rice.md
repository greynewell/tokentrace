## Ingredients

- 4 slices bacon
- 3 (6 ounce) pork chops
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 1 cup rice
- ½ cup diced onion
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- ½ teaspoon dried rosemary
- ½ teaspoon dried thyme

## Instructions

1. Cook bacon in a large skillet over medium heat until crisp, about 10 minutes.
2. Remove the bacon slices to a plate lined with paper towels to cool, retaining bacon drippings in the skillet.
3. Crumble the cooled bacon.
4. Lay pork chops into the reserved bacon drippings in the skillet; season with salt and black pepper.
5. Cook pork chops until completely browned, 3 to 5 minutes.
6. Remove pork chops to the paper towel-lined plate.
7. Stir rice, onion, and garlic into the drippings remaining in the skillet.
8. Cook and stir until the onions are tender and the rice is slightly browned, 5 to 7 minutes.
9. Pour chicken broth over the rice mixture; add crumbled bacon and cayenne pepper.
10. Bring the mixture to a boil.
11. Return the pork chops to the skillet.
12. Sprinkle rosemary and thyme over the pork chops.
13. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook at a simmer until the broth is absorbed into the rice and the pork chops are no longer pink in the middle, about 20 minutes.
