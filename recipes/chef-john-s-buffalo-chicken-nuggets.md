## Ingredients

- 1 ½ pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs
- 2 tablespoons fine dry breadcrumbs
- 1 ½ teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- ½ teaspoon seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®)
- ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1 tablespoon Buffalo-style hot pepper sauce (such as Frank's® RedHot)
- 1 tablespoon melted butter
- ½ cup cornstarch
- ⅓ cup self-rising flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt

## Instructions

1. Place chicken thighs in bowl of a food processor.
2. Add breadcrumbs, kosher salt, paprika, pepper, seafood seasoning, and cayenne pepper.
3. Drizzle in hot sauce and melted butter.
4. Pulse on and off until mixture becomes a coarse paste with very few big chunks, about 18 pulses.
5. Line a rimmed baking sheet with a silicon mat.
6. Scoop out 24 portions of the chicken mixture with a small ice-cream scoop and place on mat.
7. Cover sheet with plastic wrap.
8. Refrigerate until thoroughly chilled. (Or place pan in freezer for 15 minutes.) Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
9. Place cornstarch, self-rising flour, and salt into a quart-size zip-top plastic bag.
10. Shake bag to mix thoroughly.
11. Place 3 or 4 scoops of chilled chicken mixture into the bag; shake gently to coat and place on parchment-lined pan.
12. Repeat with the rest of the nuggets.
13. Shape them into flattened ovals, about 5/8-inch thick.
14. Refrigerate pan overnight or until starch on surface is absorbed, flipping over once while they're in the fridge.
15. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C).
16. Fry nuggets in batches for 1 minute.
17. Drain on a rack over a baking sheet; chill about 1 hour or until you're ready to serve them.
18. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
19. Fry the nuggets a second time in batches for 2 minutes.
20. Drain on rack.
