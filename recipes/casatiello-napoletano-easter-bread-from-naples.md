## Ingredients

- 1 ½ ounces fresh yeast
- 1 ½ cups warm water, divided
- 5 ⅓ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup lard, at room temperature, divided
- 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons grated pecorino Romano cheese
- 1 (4 ounce) package salami chunks
- 6  eggs

## Instructions

1. Dissolve yeast in 1/2 cup warm water.
2. Sift flour and salt onto a clean work surface; make a well in the center.
3. Add yeast mixture and 1/4 cup lard to the well.
4. Mix, adding remaining 1 cup water gradually, until dough comes together.
5. Knead until dough is soft and smooth, about 10 minutes.
6. Transfer dough to an oiled bowl.
7. Cover with a clean, damp cloth.
8. Let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 90 minutes.
9. Place dough back on the work surface.
10. Punch gently to deflate; roll into a big rectangle.
11. Brush 1/4 cup lard on top; cover with Parmesan cheese and pepper.
12. Fold dough in half to make a smaller rectangle.
13. Brush 1/4 cup lard on top; cover with pecorino Romano cheese and pepper.
14. Fold dough again into a smaller rectangle.
15. Brush remaining 1/4 cup lard on top; cover with salami chunks.
16. Cut off a small portion of the dough; reserve.
17. Roll dough into a long cylinder.
18. Place cylinder a grease ring cake mold.
19. Cover with a damp cloth.
20. Let rise in a warm place until more than doubled in volume, about 3 hours.
21. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
22. Tuck whole eggs into the ring of dough at even intervals.
23. Divide reserved dough into 12 pieces; roll into small ropes.
24. Lay 2 ropes in a cross shape over each egg to hold them in place.
25. Bake in the preheated oven until golden, about 1 hour.
26. Let cool before slicing.
