## Ingredients

- 1 ½ pounds ground beef
- 8 ounces spaghetti
- 2 (16 ounce) jars spaghetti sauce, or as needed
- 8  slices prepared garlic bread
- 1 cup chopped tomatoes
- 1 pinch dried oregano, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Cook and stir beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes; drain and discard grease.
4. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
5. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally until cooked through but firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.
6. Drain.
7. Heat spaghetti sauce in a saucepan over medium heat; stir in ground beef and cook until sauce reaches a low simmer, about 5 minutes.
8. Place garlic bread on a baking sheet and cook in the preheated oven until golden brown, 15 to 25 minutes.
9. Spread spaghetti sauce onto the plain side of each piece of garlic bread; top with tomatoes and oregano.
10. Mix remaining sauce with spaghetti and scoop spaghetti onto 1/2 of the bread slices.
11. Place the bread slices without spaghetti on top of spaghetti layer, forming a sandwich.
12. Pour any remaining sauce over sandwiches.
