## Ingredients

- 5 cups water
- 16 fluid ounces bottled clam juice
- 1 (6.5 ounce) can chopped clams, drained with juices reserved
- ¼ cup butter
- 1  yellow onion, chopped
- 2 cups Arborio rice
- ¾ cup white wine
- ½ teaspoon saffron
- 1 teaspoon dried basil
- 8 ounces cooked salmon
- kosher salt
- ground black pepper

## Instructions

1. Place water, bottled clam juice, and reserved juice from canned clams into a medium saucepan.
2. Set over medium-high heat; bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
3. In a large pot or deep skillet, melt butter over medium heat.
4. Stir in onion, and cook until softened, stirring occasionally, about 10 minutes.
5. Stir in rice, and cook, stirring, for 3 to 5 minutes.
6. Do not let rice brown.
7. Pour in wine, and cook, stirring, until the wine is evaporated.
8. Add 1/2 cup of simmering broth, and stir until the broth is almost all evaporated.
9. Then add another 1/2 cup of broth.
10. Stir in saffron and basil.
11. Continue adding broth 1/2 cup at a time and stirring until the liquid has evaporated until the rice is almost al dente, about 20 minutes.
12. When the inside of the rice is slightly more firm than you desire in the final dish, stir in clams, flaked salmon, and 1/2 cup broth.
13. Stir until the liquid evaporates.
14. Season with salt and pepper.
15. Stir in 1/2 cup broth, and stir vigorously.
16. Remove from heat before the liquid has completely evaporated.
