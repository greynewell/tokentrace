## Ingredients

- 2 (.25 ounce) packages active dry yeast
- 2 cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 6 ½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ⅓ cup butter, chilled and diced

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water.
2. Let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
3. In a large bowl, combine 4 cups of flour and salt.
4. Cut in the butter and stir in the yeast mixture.
5. Beat in the remaining flour, 1/2 cup at a time.
6. When the dough has pulled together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 10 minutes.
7. Butter a large bowl, place the dough in the bowl, and turn to coat.
8. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place until doubled in volume, about 1 hour.
9. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
10. Lightly grease a large baking sheet.
11. Deflate the dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface.
12. Form the dough into a large oval loaf.
13. Place onto the prepared pan.
14. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise until doubled in volume, about 30 minutes.
15. When the loaf is risen, cut a 1/2 inch deep cross onto the top of it.
16. Brush the top with water before baking.
17. Bake in preheated oven for 25 minutes.
18. Reduce heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until bottom of loaf sounds hollow when tapped.
19. Let cool on a wire rack before slicing.
