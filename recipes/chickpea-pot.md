## Ingredients

- 1 pound dried chickpeas (garbanzo beans)
- 4 cups water
- ¾ pound ham hocks
- 1  bay leaf
- ½ teaspoon oregano
- 1 pound Italian sausage links, cut into chunks
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 1  green bell pepper, chopped
- 1 clove garlic, crushed
- ½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 dash ground black pepper

## Instructions

1. Put chickpeas and water in a large pot; soak beans 8 hours to overnight.
2. Or, bring the beans and water to a boil in a large pot over high heat.
3. Once boiling, turn off the heat, cover, and let stand 1 hour.
4. Add ham hocks, oregano, and bay lead to the water and chickpeas; bring to a boil, place a lid on the pot, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook at a simmer until the chickpeas are tender, about 2 hours.
5. Remove and discard bay leaf.
6. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat.
7. Saute sausage chunks in hot skillet until some of the fat renders, 2 to 3 minutes; add onion, bell pepper, and garlic and saute until lightly browned, about 5 minutes.
8. Add sausage mixture to the mixture in the pot along with Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper.
9. Bring the mixture to a simmer, cover the pot, and cook until the sausage chunks are no longer pink in the center, about 20 minutes.
