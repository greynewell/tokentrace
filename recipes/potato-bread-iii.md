## Ingredients

- 1  potato, peeled and diced
- 1 ½ cups water
- 2 (.25 ounce) packages active dry yeast
- 6 ½ cups all-purpose flour
- 3 tablespoons white sugar
- 2 tablespoons shortening
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

## Instructions

1. In a medium saucepan cook potato cubes in water for about 12 minutes, or until tender.
2. Do not drain.
3. Cool mixture to 110 degrees F (43 degrees C).
4. Set aside 1/2 cup of the cooking liquid.
5. Mash potato and remaining liquid; if necessary add warm water to make 2 cups potato mixture.
6. In a large mixing bowl, sprinkle yeast on reserved 1/2 cup potato water.
7. Add mashed potato mixture, 2 cups of the flour, sugar, shortening, and salt.
8. Beat with an electric mixer on low speed  for 1/2 minute, scraping sides of bowl constantly.
9. Beat 3 minutes at high speed.
10. Stir in as much of the remaining flour as you can mix in by hand.
11. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface.
12. Knead in enough of the remaining flour to make a moderately stiff dough.
13. Knead for 6 to 8 minutes, or until dough is smooth and elastic.
14. Place in a lightly greased bowl; turn once to grease surface.
15. Cover.
16. Let rise in warm place until doubled in bulk, about 1 hour.
17. Punch down, and turn out onto a lightly floured surface.
18. Divide dough in half.
19. Cover, and  let rest for 10 minutes.
20. Shape each half into a loaf.
21. Place in greased 8 x 4 x 2 inch loaf pans.
22. Cover.
23. Let rise until nearly double, about 35 minutes.
24. Before baking, brush tops with a little water and dust with additional flour.
25. Bake at 375 degree F (190 degrees C) for 40 to 45 minutes.
26. Cover with foil for the last 15 minutes of baking to prevent over-browning.
27. Remove from pan; cool on wire rack.
