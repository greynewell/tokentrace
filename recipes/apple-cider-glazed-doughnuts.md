## Ingredients

- 3 ¼ cups all-purpose flour, or more if needed (divided)
- 2 envelopes Fleischmann's® RapidRise Yeast
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon Spice Islands® Ground Saigon Cinnamon
- 1 cup milk plus
- 2 tablespoons milk
- ¼ cup butter or margarine
- 3  egg yolks
- Mazola® Corn Oil for deep frying
- 1 cup apple cider
- 2 cups powdered sugar
- 1 tablespoon Karo® Light Corn Syrup

## Instructions

1. Combine 2 cups flour, undissolved yeast, sugar, salt and cinnamon in a large mixer bowl.
2. Heat milk and butter to very warm (120 degrees to 130 degrees F).
3. Add to flour mixture with egg yolks; beat for 2 minutes at low speed.
4. Continue adding remaining flour, until soft dough forms.
5. Knead on a lightly floured surface until smooth and elastic (about 4 to 6 minutes).
6. Cover and let rest for 10 minutes.
7. Roll out dough on a lightly floured counter into a 12-inch circle, about 1/2-inch thick.
8. Using a 3-inch cookie or biscuit cutter, cut out as many rounds as possible.
9. Cut out center with a 1-inch cookie cutter or poke hole through the center with finger.
10. Place doughnuts about 2 to 3 inches apart on lightly greased or parchment lined baking sheet.
11. Re-roll and cut remaining dough.
12. Cover doughnuts and let rise for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
13. Heat at least 2 inches of oil in a deep fryer or deep pan to 350 degrees F.
14. Fry 2 to 3 doughnuts at a time, turning occasionally until well browned, about 2 to 3 minutes.
15. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels.
16. Cool a few minutes, then transfer to wire rack.
17. Drizzle with Apple Cider Glaze OR using tongs, dunk the doughnuts in the glaze.
18. Serve warm.
19. For Apple Cider Glaze:  Boil apple cider in a small saucepan until reduced in half, about 7 to 10 minutes.
20. Place powdered sugar in medium bowl.
21. Whisk in hot cider and corn syrup until smooth.
