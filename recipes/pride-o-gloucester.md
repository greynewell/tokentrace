## Ingredients

- ¾ cup dry bread crumbs
- ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 large clove garlic, minced
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®), or to taste
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
- 2  eggs
- ¾ cup vegetable oil
- 1 pound cod or other firm white fish fillets
- 1 pinch paprika, or as needed
- 2 cups sliced onions
- 2 cups sliced green bell peppers
- 2 cups stewed tomatoes
- 1 (8 ounce) package shredded Monterey Jack cheese

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
2. Mix together the bread crumbs, Parmesan cheese, garlic, black pepper, salt, seafood seasoning, and  parsley in a shallow bowl.
3. Beat the eggs in a separate shallow bowl.
4. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
5. Dip fish fillets in the egg, then the crumbs; brush off any loose crumb mix.
6. Sprinkle fillets with paprika, and cook in the hot oil until golden brown, about 5 minutes per side.
7. Transfer the fish fillets to a 9x13-inch baking dish.
8. Cook and stir the onions and green bell peppers in the hot skillet until the onions are tender and translucent, about 10 minutes; spoon away any excess oil, and stir in the tomatoes.
9. Bring the vegetables to a boil, and spread them over the fish.
10. Cover the dish with foil.
11. Bake in the preheated oven until hot and bubbling, about 30 minutes; remove foil, and spread Monterey Jack cheese over the fish and vegetables.
12. Return to oven, and bake until the cheese is melted and beginning to brown, 8 to 10 more minutes.
