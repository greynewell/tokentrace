## Ingredients

- 1 (8 ounce) fillet whole milkfish (bangus), or to taste
- 1  lemon
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1  onion
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed
- 6 ½ tablespoons green peas
- 1  carrot, diced
- 1 stalk celery, diced
- ¼ cup raisins, or more to taste
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- vegetable oil for frying

## Instructions

1. Remove scales, gills, and insides of milkfish; cut off part of the tail.
2. Pound fish firmly to loosen it up.
3. Cut fish open lengthwise from the backside.
4. Scrape meat out into a saucepan over medium-high heat, being careful not to tear the skin.
5. Squeeze 1/2 the lemon onto the fish meat; add soy sauce.
6. Bring to a boil; cook until liquid is absorbed, about 2 minutes.
7. Remove from heat and let cool, 5 to 10 minutes.
8. Remove fish bones from the meat.
9. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
10. Add onion and garlic; saute until onion is translucent.
11. Add the fish meat, peas, carrot, celery, raisins, salt, and pepper.
12. Cook until fish is opaque and flavors are combined, about 5 minutes.
13. Let filling cool until safe to handle, at least 7 minutes.
14. Stuff fish skin with the filling.
15. Sew up any openings to seal in the filling.
16. Heat vegetable oil in a deep saucepan.
17. Fry the stuffed fish whole, turning as needed, until golden brown and crisp, 5 to 7 minutes.
18. Place on a plate or flat work surface and slice fish crosswise.
