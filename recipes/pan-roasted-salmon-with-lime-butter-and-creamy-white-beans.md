## Ingredients

- ½ cup butter
- 1  lime, zested and juiced
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- ½ teaspoon sea salt
- 4 (4 ounce) skin-on salmon fillets
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1  shallot, thinly sliced
- 2 (15 ounce) cans white beans, drained and rinsed
- ½ cup heavy whipping cream
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh chives
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh mint
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh basil
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh parsley
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh tarragon

## Instructions

1. Mix butter, lime zest, lime juice, garlic, and salt together in a bowl.
2. Spoon butter onto a large piece of plastic wrap; roll up into a log.
3. Refrigerate until firm, 30 minutes to 1 hour.
4. Season salmon with salt and pepper.
5. Heat 2 tablespoons lime butter and olive oil in a cast iron skillet set over a camping stove.
6. Cook salmon fillets, skin-side down, until crisp, about 3 minutes.
7. Flip and cook until easily flaked with a fork, 1 to 2 minutes more.
8. Transfer salmon fillets to a plate; top each fillet with 1 tablespoon lime butter.
9. Heat 1 teaspoon lime butter in the skillet.
10. Cook and stir shallot in the hot butter until softened, about 3 minutes.
11. Stir in white beans and heavy cream; season with salt and pepper.
12. Simmer until slightly thickened, 5 to 10 minutes.
13. Remove from heat and stir in chives, mint, basil, parsley, and tarragon.
14. Divide bean mixture among 4 serving plates; place 1 salmon fillet on top.
