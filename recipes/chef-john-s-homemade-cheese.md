## Ingredients

- 1 quart whole milk
- 1 cup cultured buttermilk
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice, or as needed
- ¾ teaspoon salt

## Instructions

1. Heat milk in a heavy-bottomed saucepan over low heat, stirring regularly, to 175 degrees F (79 degrees C).
2. Pour buttermilk and lemon juice into milk and remove from heat.
3. Stir gently with a wooden spoon; the curds and whey will begin to separate.
4. If mixture does not curdle, add another teaspoon of lemon juice.
5. Let milk mixture sit for 10 minutes.
6. Place a colander over a stockpot and line the colander with 4 layers of cheesecloth.
7. Ladle cheese curds into prepared colander.
8. Let drain for 5 minutes.
9. Gather the cheesecloth and tie at the top with twine or string.
10. Tie cheesecloth bag to a wooden spoon and hang it over the stockpot.
11. Let drain for 30 minutes.
12. Cut off tied top of cheesecloth.
13. Unwrap the drained cheese and turn out into a bowl.
14. Mix salt into cheese with a wooden spoon.
15. Transfer cheese to a ramekin, cover ramekin with plastic wrap, and refrigerate 8 hours to overnight.
