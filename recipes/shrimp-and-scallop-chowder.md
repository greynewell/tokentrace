## Ingredients

- 4 cups water
- 2 cups uncooked white rice
- 3 (10 ounce) cans clam juice, divided
- ¼ cup butter, divided
- 1 small sweet onion (such as Vidalia®), diced
- 1 thick slice honey ham, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3  potatoes, peeled and cut into bite-size pieces
- ¼ teaspoon salt, or to taste
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper, or to taste
- ½ pound uncooked medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
- ½ pound small sea scallops
- 1 (10 ounce) can sweet corn, drained
- ¼ teaspoon garlic powder, or to taste
- ¼ teaspoon onion powder, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Bring water and rice to a boil in a saucepan.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and liquid has been absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes.
3. Place rice and 1/2 can clam juice in a blender; puree until smooth.
4. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a large stock pot over medium heat.
5. Add onion, ham, and garlic; cook and stir until garlic starts to pop, about 10 minutes.
6. Mix in potatoes, salt, and pepper.
7. Add the remaining 2 1/2 cans clam juice.
8. Cover the chowder base and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 15 minutes.
9. Dice shrimp into small pieces.
10. Add shrimp and scallops to the chowder base.
11. Cook until shrimp turns pink and scallops are opaque, about 5 minutes.
12. Stir in the rice puree, the remaining 2 tablespoons butter, corn, garlic powder, and onion powder.
13. Reduce heat to low.
14. Simmer, covered, until flavors combine, about 5 minutes.
