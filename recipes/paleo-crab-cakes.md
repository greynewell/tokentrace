## Ingredients

- 1  large egg
- 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
- 1 tablespoon red pepper flakes
- 1 ½ teaspoons seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®)
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- ½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- ¼ teaspoon hot pepper sauce (such as Tabasco®)
- ¼ teaspoon lemon juice
- ground black pepper to taste
- 1 pound fresh lump crabmeat
- ¼ cup almond flour
- 1 tablespoon minced parsley
- 1 teaspoon sliced green onion
- ⅓ cup almond flour for dredging

## Instructions

1. Grease a baking dish.
2. Beat egg, mayonnaise, red pepper flakes, seafood seasoning, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, hot pepper sauce, lemon juice, and black pepper together in a bowl until smooth.
3. Place crabmeat into a separate bowl, trying to not break too many of the big chunks; add 1/4 cup almond flour, parsley, and green onion.
4. Pour egg mixture over the crabmeat mixture and gently mix until dry ingredients are completely moistened; shape into 12 cakes.
5. Spread 1/3 cup almond flour into a wide, shallow bowl.
6. Roll cakes in almond flour to coat and arrange into the prepared baking dish.
7. Refrigerate crab cakes for at least 1 hour.
8. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
9. Bake in preheated oven until hot in the center and golden brown on the outside, 15 to 20 minutes.
