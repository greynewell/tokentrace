## Ingredients

- 1 ½ cups whole wheat flour, divided
- 1 cup water
- ½ cup sourdough starter
- ½ cup amaranth flour
- 2 tablespoons honey
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour, or more as needed
- 3 tablespoons cornmeal, or as needed

## Instructions

1. Combine 1 cup whole wheat flour, water, and sourdough starter in a large bowl.
2. Stir thoroughly and cover with a clean towel.
3. Let sit until spongy, 7 hours to 1 day.
4. Stir remaining 1/2 cup whole wheat flour, amaranth flour, honey, baking soda, and salt into the starter mixture.
5. Mix in all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup at a time, until the dough is no longer sticky.
6. Knead gently until dough is smooth and elastic.
7. Roll out dough on a floured work surface to 1/2-inch thickness.
8. Cut into rounds with a 2 1/2-inch biscuit cutter.
9. Sprinkle some cornmeal over a baking sheet.
10. Transfer rounds to the baking sheet; sprinkle tops with remaining cornmeal.
11. Cover with a clean towel and let rise until puffy, about 45 minutes.
12. Heat a large skillet over medium heat.
13. Place rounds into hot skillet and lower heat to medium-low.
14. Cook until golden brown and edges look dry, about 6 minutes per side.
15. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
