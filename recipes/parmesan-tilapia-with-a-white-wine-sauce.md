## Ingredients

- ¼ cup all-purpose flour
- ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- ½ teaspoon dried thyme
- ½ teaspoon dried dill weed
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ½ cup milk
- ½ cup all-purpose flour
- 4 (4 ounce) tilapia fillets
- 2 tablespoons butter
- ¼ cup dry white wine
- ¼ cup milk

## Instructions

1. Mix together 1/4 cup flour, Parmesan cheese, thyme, dill, and salt in a shallow dish.
2. Pour 1/2 cup milk and 1/2 cup flour in two separate shallow dishes.
3. Dip tilapia fillets into plain flour, then milk.
4. Thoroughly coat the fillets with the seasoned flour; reserve the remaining seasoned flour.
5. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
6. Brown the fillets in the butter, about 2 minutes each side.
7. Reduce heat to medium-low and continue to cook until the fish is easily flaked with a fork, 2 to 5 minutes more.
8. Transfer the fillets to a dish.
9. Increase heat to medium, and pour the remaining seasoned flour into the skillet.
10. Whisk in white wine; cook and stir until reduced and thickened, about 5 minutes.
11. Gradually stir in 1/4 cup milk.
12. Reduce heat to low and allow sauce to simmer for 1 or 2 minutes more.
13. Spoon sauce over tilapia to serve.
