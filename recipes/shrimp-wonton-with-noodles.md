## Ingredients

- 2 (8 ounce) packages thick rice noodles (he fun)
- ½ pound raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
- 1 (16 ounce) package wonton wrappers
- 1  egg white, beaten
- 2 (14 ounce) cans chicken broth
- 2 cups water
- 2 tablespoons chopped scallions

## Instructions

1. Soak rice noodles in a bowl of cold water until softened, about 10 minutes.
2. Drain.
3. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
4. Add noodles; cook, stirring occasionally, until tender, about 5 minutes.
5. Drain.
6. Place shrimp on a flat work surface; pound with a meat tenderizer until a thick paste forms.
7. Mix in sesame oil, salt, and pepper.
8. Place a wonton wrapper diagonally on a flat work surface.
9. Add a spoonful of shrimp paste above the center of the wrapper.
10. Dip a finger in egg white and dab it on the top corner.
11. Fold top corner over to just cover the shrimp paste.
12. Dab egg white on the fold; overlap left and right corners in the center.
13. Repeat with remaining wrappers and shrimp.
14. Bring chicken broth and water to a boil in a large pot.
15. Add wontons.
16. Cook over medium-high heat until wontons float to the surface, about 5 minutes.
17. Divide noodles among serving bowls; sprinkle scallions on top.
18. Divide wontons among bowls.
19. Cover with hot broth.
