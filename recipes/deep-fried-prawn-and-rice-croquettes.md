## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons butter
- ½  onion, finely chopped
- 4 ounces peeled shrimp, chopped
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon white wine
- ½ cup milk
- 2 tablespoons milk
- 2 cups cooked rice
- 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon chopped fresh dill, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ground black pepper
- vegetable oil for frying
- 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour, or as needed
- 1  egg
- ½ cup bread crumbs

## Instructions

1. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
2. Add onion; cook and stir until softened and browned, about 5 minutes.
3. Add shrimp; cook and stir until opaque, 1 to 2 minutes.
4. Stir in 2 tablespoons flour and white wine.
5. Pour in milk gradually, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon; cook until white sauce thickens, 3 to 5 minutes.
6. Remove white sauce from heat.
7. Stir in rice until evenly mixed.
8. Add Parmesan cheese, dill, salt, and black pepper.
9. Cool until mixture is easily handled, about 15 minutes.
10. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
11. Place 3 tablespoons flour in a shallow bowl.
12. Whisk egg in a small bowl.
13. Spread bread crumbs on a plate.
14. Form rice mixture into 8 equal balls.
15. Roll 1 ball in flour, dip in beaten egg, and coat with bread crumbs.
16. Repeat with remaining balls.
17. Deep-fry in hot oil until golden brown, about 3 minutes.
