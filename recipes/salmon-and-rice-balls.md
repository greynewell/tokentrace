## Ingredients

- ½ cup uncooked white rice
- ½ cup water
- 2 (14.75 ounce) cans salmon, drained and flaked
- ½ cup grated carrot  (Optional)
- ¼ cup chopped onion
- 2  eggs
- salt and pepper to taste
- 1 (10.75 ounce) can  condensed cream of mushroom soup
- ½ cup water

## Instructions

1. Bring the rice and 1/2 cup water to a boil in a saucepan over high heat.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low, and cover; simmer until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes.
3. Fluff with fork, and allow to cool.
4. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
5. Spray an 11 x 9 baking pan with cooking spray.
6. Remove the skin from the salmon (and bones if you prefer).
7. Combine salmon, optional carrots, onions, eggs, cooled rice, and salt and pepper in a large bowl.
8. Mix well with your hands.
9. Form into 8 (tennis ball sized) balls, using about 3/4 cup salmon mixture per portion.
10. Arrange balls in the prepared pan, allowing room for them to expand.
11. Mix the soup and 1/2 cup water together in a small bowl; pour over the salmon balls.
12. Cover with foil.
13. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 hour.
14. Allow salmon balls to rest for a few minutes before serving.
