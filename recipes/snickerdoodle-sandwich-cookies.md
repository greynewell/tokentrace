## Ingredients

- 1 sheet Reynolds® Parchment Paper
- 1 cup butter, softened
- 1 ¾ cups sugar, divided
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 teaspoon cream of tartar
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- 2  large eggs eggs
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- ½ cup chopped pecans, toasted
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- ⅔ cup butter, softened
- 8 cups powdered sugar, divided, or more as needed
- ¼ cup milk, plus more as needed
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1 teaspoon vanilla

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
2. Line two cookie sheets with Reynolds® Parchment Paper.
3. Beat the butter with an electric mixer on medium to high speed for 30 seconds in a large mixing bowl.
4. Add 1 1/2 cups sugar, baking soda, cream of tartar, and salt.
5. Beat until combined, scraping the sides of the bowl occasionally.
6. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating after each addition just until combined.
7. Beat in the vanilla.
8. Beat in as much of the flour as you can with the mixer.
9. Using a wooden spoon, stir in any remaining flour and the pecans.
10. Cover and chill about 1 hour or until the dough is easy to handle.
11. Combine 1/4 cup sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a small bowl.
12. Shape dough into 1 1/2-inch balls.
13. Roll the balls in the sugar mixture to coat.
14. Arrange 2 inches apart on the prepared cookie sheets.
15. Flatten the dough balls slightly.
16. Bake 8 to 10 minutes or until edges are firm and bottoms are very light brown.
17. Let cool on the cookie sheets for 2 minutes.
18. Slide the parchment paper with baked cookies on it to wire racks and let cool completely.
19. Once cooled, slide the parchment paper onto a work surface.
20. Spread Cinnamon Buttercream Frosting onto the flat side of half of the cookies.
21. Top with remaining cookies, flat-sides down.
22. Cinnamon Buttercream Frosting: Beat butter with an electric mixer on medium speed in a large mixing bowl until smooth.
23. Add 2 cups powdered sugar, beating well.
24. Beat in 1/4 cup milk, cinnamon, and vanilla.
25. Gradually beat in 6 cups more powdered sugar.
26. Beat in 2 to 4 teaspoons more milk until the frosting is of spreading consistency.
