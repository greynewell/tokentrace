## Ingredients

- 2 cups self-rising flour
- ⅛ teaspoon baking soda
- ¾ teaspoon salt
- 1 ½ tablespoons white sugar
- 2 tablespoons shortening
- 2 tablespoons butter, softened
- 1 ¼ cups buttermilk
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 ½ tablespoons butter, melted

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 475 degrees F (245 degrees C).
2. Spray a 10 1/2 inch  cast iron skillet with non stick cooking spray.
3. In a medium bowl, combine flour, baking soda, salt and sugar.
4. Cut in the shortening and softened butter with a fork until the lumps are pea size.
5. Gently stir in the buttermilk until all of the flour is incorporated; do not over mix.
6. The dough should be very wet.
7. If not, add more buttermilk to achieve a sloppy consistency.
8. Let stand for 2 to 3 minutes.
9. Place all-purpose flour in a bowl.
10. Spray an ice cream scoop or large spoon with vegetable spray and scoop out dough.
11. Drop each biscuit one at a time into the flour.
12. Using well-floured hands, pick up each piece and pass from hand to hand lightly to shake off excess flour and shape it into a soft round.
13. Place the biscuits gently into cast iron skillet.
14. They will be a tight fit.
15. Do not mash the biscuits down!  Brush tops lightly with melted butter.
16. Bake at 475 degrees F (245 degrees C) for 16 to 18 minutes.
17. Cool before serving.
