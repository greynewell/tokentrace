## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons agave syrup
- 2 cups warm water
- 2 ½ teaspoons active dry yeast
- 3  eggs
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 cups quinoa flour
- 1 cup sorghum flour
- 1 cup tapioca starch
- ½ teaspoon sea salt
- ½ teaspoon xanthan gum
- ¾ cup sunflower seeds

## Instructions

1. Whisk agave syrup into warm water in a small bowl; add yeast.
2. Let stand until yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam, about 5 minutes.
3. Whisk in eggs and oil.
4. Combine quinoa flour, sorghum flour, tapioca starch, salt, and xanthan gum in the bowl of a stand mixer.
5. With the mixer running on low speed, pour in yeast mixture.
6. Mix until dough is smooth, 2 to 3 minutes.
7. Add sunflower seeds; mix until incorporated.
8. Line an 8 1/2x4 1/2-inch loaf pan with parchment paper; pour in dough.
9. Cover with plastic wrap.
10. Let rise in a warm, draft-free space, 30 to 45 minutes.
11. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
12. Bake loaf in the preheated oven until browned and an instant-read thermometer inserted into the center reads 210 degrees F (99 degrees C), 50 to 60 minutes.
13. Invert loaf onto a wire rack and cool completely, about 1 hour, before slicing.
