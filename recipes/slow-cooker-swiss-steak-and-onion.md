## Ingredients

- ¾ cup all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 teaspoons dry mustard
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 pounds cube steak
- ¼ cup vegetable oil
- 1  onion, sliced thin
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 cups beef broth
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 (14.5 ounce) cans diced tomatoes
- 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 2 teaspoons ground paprika
- 2 teaspoons ground thyme
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano

## Instructions

1. Mix together flour, salt, mustard, and pepper in a wide, shallow bowl. Add cube steaks to flour mixture; toss to coat completely.
2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry steaks in hot oil until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side; transfer to a slow cooker, reserving drippings in the skillet.
3. Sauté onion and garlic in drippings in the skillet over medium heat until softened, 2 to 3 minutes; add to the slow cooker. Discard oil.
4. Stir together broth and tomato paste in a large bowl until paste is dissolved; add tomatoes, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, thyme, and oregano. Pour broth mixture over steaks in the slow cooker.
5. Cook on Low until meat is tender and falling apart, 6 to 8 hours.
