## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1  onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, diced
- 2 pounds plum tomatoes, peeled, seeded, and chopped
- 1  red bell pepper, seeded and chopped
- 1 cup tomato puree
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 cube fish bouillon
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
- freshly ground black pepper to taste
- 2  bay leaves
- 20  pitted green olives
- 2 tablespoons capers, rinsed
- ¼ cup butter
- 8 large white fish fillets
- 6  pickled banana peppers, sliced

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
2. Grease a rectangular baking dish large enough to fit the fish fillets in a single layer.
3. Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
4. Cook and stir onion and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
5. Stir in plum tomatoes and red bell pepper; simmer until softened, about 5 minutes.
6. Stir in tomato puree; cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
7. Stir salt, bouillon cube, oregano, cinnamon, pepper, and bay leaves into the saucepan.
8. Cook until flavors combine, about 10 minutes.
9. Add olives and capers; simmer for 5 minutes.
10. Remove sauce from heat.
11. Melt butter in a separate skillet over medium heat.
12. Season fish fillets with salt and pepper; cook in the hot butter until browned, 1 to 2 minutes per side.
13. Lay fish fillets in the baking dish in a single layer; cover with sauce.
14. Arrange sliced peppers on top.
15. Cover baking dish tightly with aluminum foil.
16. Bake in the preheated oven until fish flakes easily with a fork, 10 to 15 minutes.
