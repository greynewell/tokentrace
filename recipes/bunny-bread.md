## Ingredients

- 2 (1 pound) loaves frozen bread dough, thawed
- 2  raisins
- 2  sliced almonds
- 1  egg, beaten
- 6  lettuce leaves

## Instructions

1. Grease a baking sheet.
2. Cut off 1/4 of the dough from 1 loaf and form into a pear-shaped ball for the bunny head.
3. Form the remaining 3/4 loaf into a fat oval shape for the body.
4. Place the body onto the prepared baking sheet and attach the head by pinching the 2 pieces together.
5. Make narrow cuts, about 3/4 in deep, on each side of head for whiskers.
6. Cut second loaf of dough into 4 equal pieces.
7. Roll 2 pieces into 16-inch ropes.
8. Fold each rope in half, and pinch the ends to the head to form ears.
9. Cut the third piece of dough in half and shape into 2 ovals about 3 1/2 inches long for back paws.
10. Attach the paws to the bottom sides of the body.
11. Make two 1-inch cuts on the paws for toes.
12. Cut the remaining dough into 3 pieces.
13. Form 2 pieces into balls and pinch on sides for front paws.
14. Divide the remaining piece of dough into 3 equal pieces and roll into 1-inch balls.
15. Press the dough balls onto the  the face to form two cheeks and a nose.
16. Press raisins onto the face to make eyes.
17. Press almond slices close together onto face to make bunny teeth.
18. Cover and let dough rise in a warm place until doubled, 30 to 45 minutes.
19. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
20. Brush the loaf lightly with beaten egg.
21. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, 25 to 30 minutes.
22. Remove to a wire rack to cool.
23. To serve, line a serving platter with lettuce leaves and place the bunny in the center.
