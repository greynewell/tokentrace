## Ingredients

- cooking spray
- 2 (18.3 ounce) packages fudge brownie mix (such as Duncan Hines®)
- 1 cup vegetable oil
- 4  eggs
- ½ cup water
- 6 ounces semisweet chocolate chips
- ½ cup heavy whipping cream
- 1 (16 ounce) package confectioners' coating (such as Wilton® Candy Melts®)
- lollipop sticks

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
2. Coat an 11x15-inch baking pan with cooking spray.
3. Empty brownie mix into a large bowl.
4. Add vegetable oil, eggs, and water; stir with a wooden spoon until batter is well blended.
5. Pour batter into the prepared baking pan.
6. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted 1 inch from the edge of the pan comes out clean, 35 to 40 minutes.
7. Let cool completely, about 30 minutes.
8. Combine chocolate chips and heavy cream in a microwave-safe bowl.
9. Heat in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring after each interval, until melted and smooth.
10. Cool, about 5 minutes.
11. Break brownies into pieces and place in a large bowl.
12. Pour ganache evenly over brownie pieces; mix thoroughly.
13. Line a jelly roll pan with parchment paper.
14. Press brownie mixture evenly into the pan.
15. Freeze until firm, about 30 minutes.
16. Pour confectioners' coating into a microwave-safe bowl.
17. Microwave at 50 percent power for 1 minute; stir thoroughly.
18. Continue to microwave and stir at 30-second intervals until smooth and completely melted, 1 to 2 minutes more.
19. Roll brownie mixture into balls; insert a lollipop stick halfway into each.
20. Dip balls one at a time into melted confectioners' coating to form a thin, even coating, letting the excess drip off.
21. Stick into a styrofoam block; let stand until coating hardens, about 10 minutes.
