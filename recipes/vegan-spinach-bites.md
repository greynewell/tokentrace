## Ingredients

- 1 pound Idaho potatoes, peeled and cut into 2-inch chunks
- 2 bunches fresh spinach, divided
- 3 tablespoons canola oil, divided, or more as needed
- 1  onion, minced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons water
- 1 tablespoon flaxseed meal
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ cup all-purpose flour

## Instructions

1. Place potatoes into a large pot and cover with water; bring to a boil.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, about 20 minutes.
3. Drain and transfer to a large bowl.
4. Allow to cool to room temperature, about 30 minutes.
5. Run 1/2 cup spinach through a juicer according to manufacturer's instructions; add to cooled potatoes and mash until smooth.
6. Pulse remaining spinach in a food processor until finely chopped.
7. Add to mashed potatoes.
8. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
9. Add onion and garlic; cook and stir until fragrant, about 5 minutes.
10. Let cool slightly, about 10 minutes.
11. Stir into mashed potatoes.
12. Mix water and flaxseed meal together in a small bowl; let sit until thickened, about 5 minutes.
13. Add to mashed potatoes with baking powder and salt; mix to combine.
14. Form into patties.
15. Dust both sides of patties generously with flour.
16. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
17. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
18. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
19. Cook patties in batches until a pale crust forms, about 3 minutes per side.
20. Repeat with remaining 1 tablespoon oil and patties.
21. Transfer pan-fried patties to baking sheet.
22. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown and cooked through, about 10 minutes.
