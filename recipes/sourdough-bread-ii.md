## Ingredients

- 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
- ½ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 2 cups bread flour
- 2 cups warm water
- 1 tablespoon white sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
- ½ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 1 ½ cups sourdough starter
- 2 teaspoons white sugar
- 1 ½ teaspoons salt
- 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
- 5 ½ cups bread flour
- 1 tablespoon butter, melted

## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, dissolve 2 1/4 teaspoons yeast in 1/2 cup warm water.
2. Stir in 2 cups flour, 2 cups lukewarm water, 1 tablespoon sugar, and 1 teaspoon salt.
3. Beat smooth with rotary beater.
4. Let stand 3 to 5 days uncovered at room temperature stirring 2 or 3 times a day.
5. Cover at night to prevent drying.
6. In a large bowl, dissolve 2 1/4 teaspoons yeast in 1/2 cup warm water.
7. Blend in starter batter, 2 teaspoons sugar, and 1 1/2 teaspoons salt.
8. Add 3 1/2 cups flour, and beat with a rotary beater for 3 to 4 minutes.
9. Cover, and let rise till double in bulk.
10. Mix soda with remaining flour; gradually mix into risen dough, adding enough to make the dough stiff.
11. Turn out onto a floured board, and knead 8 to 10 minutes.
12. Shape into one large or 2 medium loaves, and place on a lightly greased baking sheet.
13. With a sharp knife, make diagonal cuts across top of dough.
14. Let rise till double.
15. Bake at 400 degrees F (205 degrees C).
16. Remove medium loaves after 35 to 40 minutes, large loaf after 40 to 45 minutes.
17. Brush with melted butter and let cool before serving.
