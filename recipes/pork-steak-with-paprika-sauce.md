## Ingredients

- 1 pound pork steak
- ½ cup all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons ground paprika
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- ¼ cup butter
- 3  tomatoes, finely chopped
- 1 cube beef bouillon
- ¾ cup water, divided
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley

## Instructions

1. Place pork steak between 2 sheets of heavy plastic on a solid, level surface.
2. Firmly pound with the smooth side of a meat mallet to 1/2-inch thickness.
3. Pat dry with a paper towel.
4. Mix flour, paprika, salt, and ground black pepper on a plate.
5. Measure out 1 tablespoon of the mixture and set aside.
6. Dredge pork in remaining mixture, coating both sides.
7. Melt butter in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat.
8. Add pork; cook until browned, about 3 minutes per side.
9. Transfer to a plate.
10. Add tomatoes to the skillet; cook and stir until softened, about 3 minutes.
11. Dissolve beef bouillon cube in 1/2 cup water in a small bowl.
12. Stir into the tomatoes.
13. Cook, covered, until sauce thickens, 2 to 3 minutes.
14. Return pork steak to the skillet.
15. Reduce heat and simmer until pork is tender and reaches an internal temperature of 145 degrees F (63 degrees C), about 15 minutes.
16. Transfer to a serving dish.
17. Mix reserved 1 tablespoon flour mixture with remaining 1/4 cup water in a small bowl.
18. Stir into the sauce in the skillet.
19. Cook and stir until thickened, about 4 minutes.
20. Pour sauce over pork and garnish with parsley.
