## Ingredients

- 2 medium heads cauliflower, chopped
- 4 cups chicken broth, divided
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 stalks celery, chopped
- 1 small yellow onion, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 5 large carrots, chopped
- 1 pound cooked chicken, shredded
- 1 tablespoon dried parsley
- 2 teaspoons dried rosemary
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 ¼ cups water
- 1 (8 ounce) package uncooked wild rice

## Instructions

1. Combine cauliflower and 2 cups chicken broth in a saucepan over medium-low heat; cook until tender, about 20 minutes.
2. Puree cauliflower and broth in batches in a blender; set aside.
3. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
4. Add celery, onion, and garlic.
5. Cook, stirring occasionally, until onions are slightly translucent, 5 to 7 minutes.
6. Add carrots and cook until they are slightly tender, 5 to 10 minutes longer.
7. Add cooked chicken, remaining chicken broth, parsley, and rosemary; bring to a boil.
8. Add pureed cauliflower mixture and season with salt and pepper.
9. Cover and simmer over low heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
10. While the soup is simmering, bring water to a boil in a medium saucepan.
11. Stir in rice and cover with a tight-fitting lid.
12. Reduce heat and cook at a fast simmer until rice is tender, 45 to 50 minutes.
13. Remove from the heat and keep covered so rice can steam, 10 minutes.
14. Fluff with a fork and add to the soup.
