## Ingredients

- 2 (.25 ounce) packages active dry yeast
- 2 tablespoons white sugar
- 1 ½ cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 1 cup milk
- ½ cup butter
- 1 cup white sugar
- 2  eggs
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 8 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons butter, melted

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and 2 tablespoons sugar in warm water.
2. Let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
3. Warm the milk in a small saucepan until it bubbles, then remove from heat.
4. Mix in the butter and remaining 1 cup sugar; stir until melted.
5. Let cool until lukewarm.
6. In a large bowl, combine the yeast mixture with the milk mixture, eggs, salt and 3 cups of flour; stir well to combine.
7. Stir in the remaining flour, 1/2 cup at a time, beating well after each addition.
8. When the dough has pulled together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 8 minutes.
9. Place in a greased bowl, turning once to grease the top.
10. Cover and let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 1 hour.
11. Punch down dough and divide into thirds; divide each portion in a 14 inch roll; divide each roll into 14 pieces.
12. Roll the pieces into 9 inch ropes and tie into knots.
13. Place the rolls 2 inches apart on greased baking sheets.
14. Cover and let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 45 minutes.
15. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
16. Bake in preheated oven) for 15 minutes until lightly browned.
17. Remove from oven and brush with melted butter.
