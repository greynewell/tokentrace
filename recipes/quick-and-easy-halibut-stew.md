## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 6 ounces Spanish-style chorizo, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 bulb fennel, trimmed and thinly sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- ⅔ cup white wine
- 2 (14.5 ounce) cans diced tomatoes, with juice
- 2 (8 ounce) bottles clam juice
- 24 ounces halibut, skin removed, cut into 1-inch pieces
- salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley, or to taste  (Optional)

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat until hot.
2. Cook chorizo, onion, and fennel until onion is soft and translucent and chorizo is browned, about 8 minutes.
3. Stir in garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
4. Pour in wine and bring to a boil while scraping the browned bits of food of the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon, about 2 minutes.
5. Stir in tomatoes with their juice and clam juice.
6. Bring to a simmer and cook until flavors meld, about 10 minutes.
7. Season halibut with salt and pepper and place in tomato mixture, coving it with sauce.
8. Bring to a simmer.
9. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until fish flakes easily with a fork and is cooked through, about 5 minutes.
10. Season with salt and pepper and sprinkle with parsley.
