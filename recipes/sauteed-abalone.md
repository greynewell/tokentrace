## Ingredients

- 1 (16 ounce) package linguine pasta
- 2 pounds abalone without shell
- 3  limes, juiced
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2  green bell peppers, sliced
- 1 (16 ounce) jar Alfredo sauce

## Instructions

1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil over high heat.
2. Once the water is boiling, stir in the linguine, and return to a boil.
3. Cook the pasta uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the pasta has cooked through, but is still firm to the bite, about 11 minutes.
4. Drain well in a colander set in the sink.
5. Slice abalone into 1/2 inch slices.
6. Place the abalone between two sheets of heavy plastic (resealable freezer bags work well) on a solid, level surface.
7. Firmly pound the abalone with the smooth side of a meat mallet to a thickness of 1/4 inch.
8. Mix lime juice and garlic in a large bowl.
9. Toss abalone slices and bell pepper in the lime garlic mixture to coat.
10. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat and stir in the abalone and bell pepper, including the juice.
11. Cook and stir until the cooked through, 5 to 10 minutes.
12. Remove the abalone slices and place on a plate and set aside.
13. Continue cooking the peppers, until tender.
14. Meanwhile, pour   into a saucepan and heat over medium-low heat until hot, stirring occasionally.
15. Serve abalone over a bed of linguine and Alfredo sauce.
