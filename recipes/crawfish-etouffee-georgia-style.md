## Ingredients

- ½ cup unsalted butter
- 2 cups finely chopped sweet onions (such as Vidalia®)
- 1 cup celery root (celeriac), peeled and finely chopped
- 2 ½ tablespoons chopped green bell pepper
- 3 tablespoons chopped red bell pepper
- 2 ½ tablespoons chopped yellow bell pepper
- 1 pound peeled crawfish tails
- 2 teaspoons minced garlic
- 3  bay leaves
- 1 ½ tablespoons all-purpose flour
- ½ cup low-sodium chicken broth
- ½ cup water
- 1 teaspoon coarse smoked salt flakes
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste
- 3 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
- 3 tablespoons green onions, chopped

## Instructions

1. Melt butter in a large cast iron skillet over medium heat.
2. Stir in onion, celery root, and bell peppers; cook and stir until the onion has softened and turned translucent, 8 to 10 minutes.
3. Stir in crawfish, garlic, and bay leaves.
4. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10 to 12 minutes.
5. Whisk flour into chicken broth and water in a bowl until smooth; pour into crawfish mixture.
6. Season with smoked salt and cayenne pepper.
7. Bring mixture to a boil.
8. Reduce heat to medium; cook and stir until mixture thickens, about 4 minutes.
9. Stir in parsley and green onions; cook 2 minutes more.
