## Ingredients

- 12 ounces long grain white rice
- 3 cups water
- salt to taste
- 2 tablespoons sunflower seed oil
- 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
- 2 cloves garlic, coarsely chopped
- 1  fresh red chile pepper, seeded and chopped
- 1 tablespoon curry paste
- 1 bunch green onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon sunflower seed oil
- 2  eggs
- 2 ounces roasted peanuts, coarsely chopped
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro

## Instructions

1. Bring rice, water, and salt to a boil in a saucepan.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and liquid has been absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes.
3. Heat 2 tablespoons sunflower oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat; cook and stir chicken, garlic, and red chile pepper until chicken is golden brown, 5 to 7 minutes.
4. Stir in curry paste until fragrant, about 1 minute.
5. Add cooked rice and green onions, cooking and stirring for 5 minutes more.
6. Season with soy sauce.
7. Push rice mixture to one side of the wok.
8. Heat remaining 1 teaspoon sunflower oil in middle of wok; cook and stir eggs until just set, about 1 minute.
9. Stir rice mixture into eggs.
10. Sprinkle with peanuts and cilantro.
11. Serve immediately.
