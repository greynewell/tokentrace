## Ingredients

- 1 pound ground beef
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 5  potatoes, thinly sliced
- 1 (8 ounce) package cooked and cubed ham, or to taste
- 1 pound thick-cut maple-cured bacon
- 1 (12 ounce) bottle barbeque sauce, or to taste
- 1 (8 ounce) bottle ranch dressing, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Cook and stir beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes; drain and discard grease.
3. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
4. Cook and stir potato slices in hot oil until tender but not crispy, 10 to 20 minutes.
5. Heat remaining oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
6. Saute ham in hot oil until heated through, 3 to 7 minutes.
7. Cook bacon in a large skillet over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until evenly browned, about 10 minutes.
8. Drain and crumble bacon.
9. Whisk barbeque sauce and ranch dressing together in a bowl.
10. Stir potatoes, beef, ham, and bacon together in a large bowl; top with barbeque sauce mixture and toss to coat.
