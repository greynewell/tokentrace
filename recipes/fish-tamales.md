## Ingredients

- 50  dried corn husks
- 1 (4 ounce) can chopped roasted green chile peppers
- ¼ cup lemon juice
- 5 teaspoons hot sauce (such as Cholula®)
- 5 cloves garlic, pressed, divided
- 1 tablespoon finely minced white onion
- ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 5 pounds tilapia fillets
- 3  roasted red peppers, drained and chopped
- 5  bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- 2 teaspoons ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 2 cups coarsely chopped green cabbage
- ¼ cup finely shredded red cabbage
- 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
- 2 tablespoons whole-grain mustard
- 1 (2 pound) package masa harina
- 2 tablespoons lard
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 2 cups water
- 5 tablespoons chili powder

## Instructions

1. Soak corn husks in water for at least 1 hour.
2. Drain.
3. Combine green chiles, lemon juice, hot sauce, 1 garlic clove, and onion in a blender and blend.
4. Add olive oil slowly, blending sauce on low speed.
5. Place tilapia on a griddle over medium heat.
6. Top fillets with roasted red peppers and 1 bay leaf each.
7. Pour sauce evenly over the fish.
8. Add 3 garlic cloves, salt, pepper, and cayenne pepper.
9. Cook for 5 minutes; flip and cook until lightly browned, about 3 minutes more.
10. Remove sauce and reserve in a bowl.
11. Transfer tilapia to a separate bowl and mash.
12. Combine green cabbage, red cabbage, vinegar, and mustard in a bowl.
13. Combine the reserved sauce, masa, lard, and baking powder in a large bowl; mix well.
14. Add water, 1 teaspoon at a time, until the mixture is as thick as biscuit dough.
15. Divide dough into 50 equal-sized balls.
16. Flatten balls into circles using a tortilla press or 2 cutting boards and 2 sheets of waxed paper.
17. Place a circle into a soaked and drained corn husk; add 1 1/2 ounces of fish mix in a line parallel with the husk.
18. Top with a small amount of cabbage.
19. Fold sides of dough over the filling and roll the husk around the tamale, folding in 1 end after rolling.
20. Repeat with remaining husks, fish, and cabbage.
21. Combine water, chili powder, and remaining 2 garlic cloves in a saucepan with a steamer basket; bring to a boil.
22. Stand tamales in basket with the folded ends down.
23. Cover and steam for 20 minutes.
24. Remove from heat and remove tamales from husks.
