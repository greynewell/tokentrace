## Ingredients

- 1 ¼ pounds beets with greens attached
- 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 2  leeks, white and light green parts only, chopped and washed
- ½ pound celery root, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
- 1 cup 1/2-inch cubed carrots
- ¼ pound parsnips, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
- 3 cups 1/2-inch cubed boiling potatoes
- 1 tablespoon white sugar
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 ½ teaspoons salt
- ⅓ cup sour cream
- ¼ cup all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill, or to taste
- 6  hard-boiled eggs, quartered lengthwise

## Instructions

1. Trim greens and stems from beets.
2. Thoroughly wash and chop greens and stems.
3. Peel and cut beets into 1/2-inch cubes.
4. Bring beets, chicken broth, leeks, celery root, carrots, and parsnips to a boil in a large pot.
5. Add potatoes, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until vegetables are tender, 10 to 12 minutes.
6. Stir beet greens and stems into soup and simmer until greens and stems are tender, about 5 minutes.
7. Mix sugar, lemon juice, and salt into soup.
8. Stir sour cream and flour together in a bowl until smooth and thickened.
9. Whisk 1 cup soup into sour cream mixture until smooth; pour sour cream mixture into soup and stir.
10. Simmer soup until thickened slightly, about 1 minute.
11. Add dill and remove pot from heat.
12. Ladle soup into serving bowls and arrange 4 egg quarters in each bowl.
