## Ingredients

- ¾ pound uncooked shrimp, peeled and deveined, or more to taste
- 4 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 ½ teaspoons salt
- ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
- ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 2 cloves garlic, sliced
- 1 small onion, chopped
- ½  yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 1  jalapeno pepper, minced  (Optional)
- 1 (12 ounce) can diced tomatoes
- 2 sprigs parsley, chopped, or more to taste, divided
- 3 leaves fresh basil, chopped, or more to taste, divided
- 1 teaspoon chopped fresh oregano
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 (12 ounce) package spaghetti, or as needed

## Instructions

1. Place shrimp on a work surface.
2. Slice each shrimp horizontally through the middle, being careful not to cut all the way through to the other side.
3. Open the 2 sides and spread them out like butterfly wings.
4. Mix 2 tablespoons oil, minced garlic, salt, red pepper flakes, and cayenne pepper together in a bowl.
5. Toss shrimp into the oil marinade and set aside.
6. Heat remaining 2 tablespoons oil in a pan over low heat.
7. Add sliced garlic.
8. Cook until golden and infused in the oil, about 2 minutes.
9. Remove garlic.
10. Increase heat to medium.
11. Add shrimp and marinade; cook until shrimp is pink, about 5 minutes.
12. Remove shrimp from pan.
13. Add onion, yellow bell pepper, and jalapeno to the pan; cook and stir until tender, about 10 minutes.
14. Add diced tomatoes, 1/2 the parsley, 1/2 the basil, oregano, and black pepper.
15. Cook until reduced to your desired thickness, 15 to 20 minutes.
16. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
17. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.
18. Drain.
19. Toss spaghetti noodles into the tomato-pepper sauce and stir in shrimp.
20. Sprinkle remaining parsley and basil on top.
21. Serve immediately.
