## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons butter
- 4 small shallots, minced
- ½ cup dry white wine
- ¾ cup milk
- ¼ cup heavy whipping cream
- 3 tablespoons finely chopped fresh tarragon
- salt to taste
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2  fennel, trimmed and sliced
- 1  blood orange, juiced
- 12 ounces fresh spinach
- 12  jumbo shrimp
- 12  sea scallops
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 5 tablespoons butter

## Instructions

1. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a saucepan over medium heat.
2. Cook and stir shallots until translucent, about 5 minutes.
3. Pour in white wine; simmer until reduced by almost half.
4. Strain out shallots; discard.
5. Pour milk, heavy cream, and tarragon into the saucepan; simmer until slightly thickened, about 5 minutes.
6. Season with salt.
7. Reduce heat to low and cover to keep warm.
8. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a skillet over medium heat.
9. Cook and stir fennel until slightly softened, about 5 minutes.
10. Pour in orange juice; simmer until slightly reduced, about 3 minutes.
11. Stir in spinach.
12. Reduce heat, cover skillet, and cook spinach until softened, about 5 minutes.
13. Stir well.
14. Remove from heat and keep covered.
15. Season shrimp and scallops with salt and pepper.
16. Melt 2 1/2 tablespoons of butter in a cast-iron skillet over medium heat.
17. Add scallops; cook until browned, about 2 minutes.
18. Flip and cook on the second side for 1 minute.
19. Add remaining butter and shrimp to the skillet; cook until shrimp turns pink, 3 to 4 minutes.
20. Spoon some of the cream sauce onto each serving dish.
21. Divide fennel and spinach mixture evenly among dishes.
22. Arrange 3 scallops and 3 shrimp in a spiral over the fennel and spinach mixture.
