## Ingredients

- 4 (5 ounce) salmon fillets
- 5 tablespoons fresh lemon juice, divided
- ¾ teaspoon dried dill weed
- ¾ teaspoon lemon pepper
- 10 tablespoons butter, divided
- 1  shallot, minced
- 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
- 5 tablespoons white wine, divided
- ½ cup heavy cream
- ½ cup milk
- ½ teaspoon dill weed
- 1 teaspoon parsley
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- salt and white pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Place salmon in a shallow dish, and rub with 3 tablespoons lemon juice.
2. Season with dill weed and lemon pepper.
3. Cover, and allow to stand 10 to 15 minutes.
4. Heat 2 tablespoons butter in a saucepan over medium heat, and saute shallot 2 minutes, until tender.
5. Mix in remaining lemon juice, vinegar, and 1/4 cup wine.
6. Simmer until reduced by at least 1/2.
7. Stir in cream and milk.
8. Season with dill, parsley, thyme, salt, and white pepper.
9. Cook and stir until thickened.
10. Whisk in 1/4 cup butter.
11. Set aside and keep warm.
12. Heat remaining 1/4 cup butter in a skillet over medium heat.
13. Place salmon in the skillet, skin side up, and cook 1 to 2 minutes, until seared.
14. Set salmon aside.
15. Deglaze the skillet with remaining 1 tablespoon wine, then mix in the cream sauce.
16. Return salmon to the skillet, and cook 8 minutes in the sauce, or until easily flaked with a fork.
17. Serve with the sauce.
