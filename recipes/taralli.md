## Ingredients

- 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
- 1 ½ cups water  (Optional)
- ¼ cup margarine
- 1 ½ tablespoons white sugar
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1  egg
- 5 pounds all-purpose flour, divided
- ½ cup fennel seed
- ¼ cup water  (Optional)

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in water.
2. Let stand for 5 minutes.
3. In a large bowl combine butter or margarine, sugar, salt, and egg.
4. Add yeast mixture and 1/2 of the flour and mix until smooth.
5. Stir in the remaining flour and the seeds.
6. Mix in additional water as needed to make a stiff dough.
7. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface.
8. Knead well.
9. Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, and turn once to coat surface.
10. Cover with a damp cloth and place in a warm spot to rise for about 1 1/2 hours.
11. Roll dough into short ropes about 1/2 inch thick.
12. Join ends to form donut shape.
13. Set aside to rise for a few minutes.
14. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
15. Fill a large saucepan half full of water; bring to a boil.
16. Drop taralli into water and boil for 1 minute.
17. Remove from water, letting both sides dry on a sheet of waxed paper.
18. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) on a lightly greased cookie sheet, turning a few times during baking until medium brown and crisp.
