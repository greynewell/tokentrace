## Ingredients

- 1 pound salmon fillets
- 1  lemon
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- ¼ cup olive oil, divided
- 1  onion, chopped
- 1 small green bell pepper, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2  tomatoes, skin removed and flesh chopped
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh tarragon
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh mint
- 2 tablespoons cayenne pepper
- 1 pinch white sugar
- 1  avocado, diced
- 2 sheets phyllo dough

## Instructions

1. Sprinkle salmon with lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
2. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a skillet over medium heat.
3. Add onion and green bell pepper; cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
4. Add garlic; stir.
5. Add tomatoes; cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
6. Stir in parsley, tarragon, dill, cilantro, and mint.
7. Add cayenne pepper and sugar; season with salt and pepper.
8. Stir in avocado.
9. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
10. Pour remaining 2 tablespoons oil into a bowl.
11. Place 1 sheet of phyllo dough on a cutting board, brush with oil and cover with a second sheet.
12. Place salmon on top of pastry, toward the bottom edge.
13. Top with 1 to 2 spoonfuls of the tomato-herb sauce, spreading evenly.
14. Roll the pastry over the salmon; seal edges.
15. Place on a baking sheet.
16. Bake in the preheated oven until golden, about 45 minutes.
17. Reheat remaining sauce and serve with the salmon.
