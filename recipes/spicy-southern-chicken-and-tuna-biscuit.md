## Ingredients

- 2 (16.3 ounce) cans refrigerated biscuit dough (such as Pillsbury®)
- 2  skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
- 3 (5 ounce) cans tuna (such as Chicken of the Sea®), drained
- ¼ cup mayonnaise (such as Best Foods®)
- ⅛ teaspoon whole grain mustard
- 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- ½ teaspoon paprika
- ⅛ teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 head lettuce, leaves rinsed and dried

## Instructions

1. Preheat an oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
2. Place biscuit dough on a baking sheet, about 1 to 2 inches apart.
3. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, about 11 to 15 minutes.
4. Remove from oven and allow the biscuits to cool.
5. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the chicken breasts until no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 30 minutes.
6. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
7. Remove the chicken from the skillet and dice into 1/2 inch cubes.
8. Mix tuna, mayonnaise, and mustard in a bowl until combined.
9. Stir in white pepper, cayenne pepper, paprika, and cumin.
10. Reserve 1/4 of the tuna mixture in a separate bowl.
11. Combine the remaining 3/4 portion of the tuna mixture with the chicken.
12. Split each biscuit in half and place over a bed of lettuce leaves.
13. Divide chicken and tuna mixture on top of each biscuit half.
14. Use the remaining 1/4 portion of the tuna mixture as a garnish on the lettuce leaves.
