## Ingredients

- ¼ cup olive oil
- 1  onion, diced
- 1  green bell pepper, diced
- 2 stalks celery, chopped
- 1  carrot, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, crushed and minced
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- 5 leaves fresh basil, chopped
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 (15 ounce) cans chicken broth, or more to taste
- 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
- ½ cup dry white wine
- 1 ½ pounds swordfish, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 (6.5 ounce) cans whole baby clams, undrained
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch, or as needed

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add onion, green bell pepper, celery, and carrot; cook and stir until softened, about 5 minutes.
3. Add garlic; cook and stir until fragrant, about 1 minute.
4. Season with oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
5. Pour chicken broth into the pot; simmer until celery and carrot are soft, about 20 minutes.
6. Pour in crushed tomatoes and white wine; simmer until flavors combine, about 10 minutes.
7. Stir swordfish into the pot; cook until easily flaked with a fork, about 10 minutes.
8. Pour in clams with juices; simmer until heated through, about 5 minutes.
9. Stir in cornstarch until stew thickens, 3 to 5 minutes.
