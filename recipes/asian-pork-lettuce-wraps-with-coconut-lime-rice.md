## Ingredients

- 2 ⅔ cups water
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 ⅓ cups long-grain white rice
- 1 pound ground pork
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- ½ cup diced red onion
- ½ cup diced red bell pepper
- ½ cup diced carrot
- ½ cup chopped water chestnuts
- 3 tablespoons grated fresh ginger
- ⅓ cup low-sodium teriyaki sauce
- ½ tablespoon honey
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
- ¼ cup unsweetened coconut milk
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 12  romaine leaves
- 2  limes, cut into wedges
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Bring water and salt to a boil in a saucepan over high heat.
2. Add rice.
3. Reduce heat, and simmer, covered, until water is absorbed, about 20 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, brown pork in a large skillet over medium-high heat, stirring to break up lumps, 5 to 7 minutes.
5. Transfer pork to a plate; drain grease from the skillet.
6. Heat oil in the skillet over medium-high heat.
7. Stir in onion, bell pepper, carrot, water chestnuts, and ginger; cook, stirring, until onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
8. Stir in pork, teriyaki, and honey; cook, stirring, until liquid has evaporated, 3 to 5 minutes.
9. Fluff cooked rice with a fork, then fold in 1/4 cup cilantro, coconut milk, and lime juice.
10. Top lettuce leaves with pork mixture and rice.
11. Garnish with lime wedges and chopped fresh cilantro.
