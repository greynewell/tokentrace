## Ingredients

- 3  zucchinis
- 1 ¼ teaspoons salt
- 2 ¼ cups sifted unbleached all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons baking soda
- 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
- ¼ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg
- 3  eggs
- 1 ⅓ cups white sugar
- 1 cup vegetable oil
- 2 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 ½ cups chopped walnuts
- 1 cup golden raisins

## Instructions

1. Soak raisins in warm water for 30 minutes.
2. Drain thoroughly in a colander or strainer; set aside.
3. Clean the zucchini and trim off the ends.
4. Do not peel.
5. Shred with a medium sized shredder.
6. You should have about 4 cups when finished.
7. Sprinkle the zucchini with 1 teaspoon of the salt and place on a plate.
8. Mix it with your hands to evenly distribute the salt.
9. Place a heavy saucepan on top of the zucchini and allow it to sit for 20 minutes to purge itself of liquid.
10. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
11. Grease and flour two 8x4 inch loaf pans and set aside.
12. Set aside 2 tablespoons of the flour.
13. Sift together the remaining flour, soda, spices, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt.
14. Beat eggs on medium speed for about 3 minutes.
15. Add the sugar, a little at a time and beat for a good 5 minutes.
16. The mixture should be light in color and thick.
17. While beating, slowly pour in the oil.
18. Empty the zucchini into a colander and squeeze to release the juices.
19. Add the vanilla to the beating eggs and then add in the zucchini.
20. Add the dry ingredients, a little at a time and mix just until the dry ingredients are mixed in.
21. Do not overmix.
22. In a small bowl combine the remaining 2 tablespoons flour, nuts, and drained raisins, and mix together to coat.
23. Fold into the batter.
24. Pour into the prepared pans.
25. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) about 1 hour or until a toothpick comes out clean.
26. Remove to a rack to cool for 10 minutes.
27. Remove from pans and cool on a rack completely.
