## Ingredients

- 2 cups milk, divided
- 10 tablespoons shortening
- 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
- ¼ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 10 tablespoons white sugar
- 4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 ½ teaspoons salt
- 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
- ½ cup margarine, melted

## Instructions

1. Place 1 cup milk with shortening in a saucepan.
2. Stir constantly over medium heat until shortening melts.
3. Do not allow to boil.
4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
5. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water.
6. Let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
7. Pour milk and margarine into a large bowl.
8. Add remaining cup of milk and dissolved yeast.
9. In a separate bowl, sift together sugar, 4 cups flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt.
10. Gradually flour mixture to milk mixture, using a wire whisk or electric mixer.
11. Place uncovered bowl on stove.
12. Allow dough to rise 2 to 2 1/2 hours until doubled in bulk.
13. Stir remaining 1 1/2 cups flour into dough and mix well.
14. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide  in half.
15. Roll out until 1/8 inch thick.
16. Brush with melted margarine and cut with biscuit cutter .
17. Fold each roll in half and dip in melted margarine, place on greased cookie sheets.
18. For immediate use continue to let rolls rise 2 hours or until doubled in bulk.
19. Refrigerate or freeze rolls if you do not plan to use at once.
20. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
21. Bake in a preheated oven for 15 to 20 minutes until golden brown.
22. When using frozen rolls, remove from freezer at least 4 hours before serving.
23. When using refrigerated rolls, allow 3 hours for rolls to rise.
