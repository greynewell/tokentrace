## Ingredients

- 2 (1 1/2 pound) whole red snappers
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 1  lime, halved
- ¼  onion, thinly sliced, or to taste
- 4 sprigs fresh basil, or to taste
- kitchen string
- ¼ cup pesto
- 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 ½ teaspoons ground fennel seeds
- 1 ½ teaspoons chili powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 cup oil for frying

## Instructions

1. Season fish cavities with salt and pepper.
2. Cut 1/2 of the lime into wedges and the other 1/2 into thin slices.
3. Fill fish with lime slices, onion, and basil.
4. Poke a hole through both sides of the cavity in order to tie it closed.
5. Push a piece of string through the holes using the handle of a spoon.
6. Tie fish shut and trim strings as needed.
7. Cut a few shallow slashes in a crosshatch pattern on both sides of the fish.
8. Cover with pesto.
9. Blend flour, fennel seeds, chili powder, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 teaspoon pepper.
10. Dredge fish in the mixture.
11. Heat 1/2 inch oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
12. Add fish.
13. Cover and cook until golden, about 8 minutes.
14. Flip and cook the other side until browned, about 8 minutes more.
15. Remove string and serve with reserved lime wedges.
