## Ingredients

- 1 cup finely chopped dried figs
- ½ cup cider vinegar
- ¼ cup brown sugar
- ¼ cup minced white onion
- ½ teaspoon ground ginger
- 1 pinch garlic powder
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper
- 1 pinch ground cloves
- 1 pinch ground cinnamon
- 1 (8 ounce) loaf French bread, cut into 1-inch slices
- olive oil cooking spray
- 1 (8 ounce) round Brie cheese, cut into bite-size pieces
- 1  Granny Smith apple, cut into matchstick-size pieces
- 1 tablespoon oregano

## Instructions

1. Combine figs, vinegar, brown sugar, onion, ginger, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, cloves, and cinnamon in a saucepan; simmer over low heat, stirring occasionally, until fig spread is reduced, about 30 minutes.
2. Transfer fig spread to a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate, 8 hours to overnight.
3. Set oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler.
4. Arrange bread slices on a baking sheet.
5. Lightly spray both sides of each bread slice with olive oil cooking spray.
6. Bake bread slices under the broiler until toasted, about 2 minutes per side.
7. Remove bread from oven and cool slightly, keeping broiler on.
8. Blend fig spread in a blender or food processor until pureed.
9. Spread about 1 teaspoon fig spread onto each toasted bread slice; top with Brie cheese and apple pieces.
10. Sprinkle each crostini with oregano.
11. Broil crostini in the preheated oven until Brie cheese is melted and browned, 1 to 2 minutes.
