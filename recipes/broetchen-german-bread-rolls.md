## Ingredients

- 2 ¼ cups whole wheat flour, or more as needed, divided
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon dried sage
- 1 cup warm water, divided
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 1 (.25 ounce) package quick-rise active dry yeast
- 2 teaspoons olive oil, divided
- 1  egg white, beaten

## Instructions

1. Mix flour, salt, and sage together in a large bowl.
2. Form a well in the center.
3. Mix 2 teaspoons warm water, honey, and yeast together in a small bowl.
4. Pour mixture into the well without mixing.
5. Cover bowl with a cloth; set in a warm area for 15 minutes.
6. Pour the remaining water and 1 teaspoon olive oil into the flour-yeast mixture; stir with a wooden spoon until dough sticks together.
7. Cover with a cloth and keep warm.
8. Let rise until doubled in volume, about 1 hour.
9. Punch down dough and turn onto a lightly floured surface.
10. Knead until smooth, using the remaining flour as needed.
11. Return to the bowl; cover and let rise until doubled in volume, about 40 minutes.
12. Grease a baking sheet with the remaining olive oil; lightly dust with flour.
13. Punch down the dough; divide into 12 equal parts.
14. Shape into ovals and cut a cross on top of each roll with a butter knife.
15. Place rolls on the prepared baking sheet.
16. Cover with a cloth and let rise until doubled in volume, about 40 minutes.
17. Brush the rolls with egg white.
18. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).
19. Bake rolls in the preheated oven until golden, about 15 minutes.
