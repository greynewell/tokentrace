## Ingredients

- 5 cloves garlic, divided
- 1 pound scallops
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
- 3 tablespoons grapeseed oil
- ¼ cup water
- ¼ cup white wine
- ½ medium onion, diced
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1  lemon, zested and juiced
- 2 tablespoons capers
- 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
- 3 sprigs fresh parsley, or as desired

## Instructions

1. Smash 2 cloves of garlic; dice or slice the other three.
2. Wash scallops and thoroughly dry on paper towels.
3. Rub smashed garlic over scallops and season with salt.
4. Heat a skillet over high heat.
5. Add 1 tablespoon butter and 1 tablespoon oil.
6. Cook scallops in batches until lightly crusted and firm, about 1 1/2 minutes per side.
7. Remove from pan and set aside.
8. Pour water and white wine into the hot skillet and bring to a boil while scraping the browned bits of food off the bottom with a wooden spoon.
9. Remove from heat.
10. Heat remaining oil in a separate pan over medium heat.
11. Add onion and season with salt and pepper; cook and stir for 1 minute.
12. Add the minced garlic and cook until mixture is lightly browned, about 5 minutes.
13. Transfer mixture to the first skillet and heat over low heat.
14. Add cream and remaining butter; heat until it starts to bubble.
15. Stir in lemon zest and juice and cook until heated through, 1 to 2 minutes.
16. Add the scallops and capers; warm for about 30 seconds.
17. Plate scallops and sauce; top with Parmesan cheese and parsley.
