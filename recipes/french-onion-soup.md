## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons butter
- 3  onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons water
- 2 ½ tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 6 ⅓ cups chicken stock
- ⅓ cup white wine
- 1 tablespoon white wine
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
- 6 (1/2 inch) slices French baguette
- ½ cup shredded Gruyère cheese

## Instructions

1. Melt butter in a large saucepan over low heat.
2. Add onions; cook, stirring constantly, until golden, 15 to 30 minutes.
3. Add water; cover and cook until onions are very tender, about 15 minutes.
4. Sprinkle flour into the saucepan; cook, stirring constantly, until onions are coated, about 5 minutes.
5. Pour in chicken stock and 1/3 cup plus 1 tablespoon white wine; season with salt and pepper.
6. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer gently until flavors combine, about 20 minutes.
7. Set oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler.
8. Toast baguette slices until very crisp, 3 to 5 minutes.
9. Divide slices among 6 oven-safe soup bowls.
10. Ladle soup on top.
11. Sprinkle Gruyère cheese over each bowl.
12. Transfer bowls to the preheated oven; broil until cheese is melted and bubbly, 3 to 5 minutes.
