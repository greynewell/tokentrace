## Ingredients

- 8  cabbage leaves
- 1 pound ground beef
- 1 ½ cups uncooked white rice
- 2 (6.5 ounce) cans tomato sauce
- 3 teaspoons New Mexico red chile powder
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ cup chopped onions
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 (10 ounce) cans diced tomatoes with green chile peppers

## Instructions

1. Freeze the cabbage leaves or blanch them for about 3 minutes in a large pot of boiling water to soften.
2. Place the ground beef, rice, tomato sauce, chile powder, garlic, onion, salt, and black pepper in a bowl.
3. Mix with your hands until combined.
4. Divide the meat mixture into 8 portions and place the meat on the softened cabbage leaves.
5. Roll the leaves up to form logs and tuck the edges under the logs.
6. Place the tamales into a pressure cooker or slow cooker.
7. Pour diced tomatoes with green chiles over tamales.
8. To cook tamales in a pressure cooker: seal the lid of the pressure cooker and bring it up to full pressure.
9. Reduce the heat to low, maintaining full pressure, and cook for 1 hour.
10. Use the quick-release function or allow the pressure to reduce naturally.
11. To cook tamales in a slow cooker: place the lid on the slow cooker and set it on High.
12. Cook the tamales for 4 hours.
