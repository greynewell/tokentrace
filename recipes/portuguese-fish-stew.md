## Ingredients

- ¼ cup olive oil
- 2  leeks, coarsely chopped
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 1 bulb fennel bulb, coarsely chopped
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 quart fish stock
- 2 cups vermouth
- 1 cup canned diced tomatoes
- 1  red bell pepper - stemmed, seeded, and diced
- 1  orange, zested
- ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes
- freshly ground pepper to taste
- 1  bay leaf
- 1 ½ pounds boneless cod fillets, cut into bite-size pieces
- ½ pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
- ½ pound clams, scrubbed
- ½ pound mussels, scrubbed

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add leeks and onion; cook and stir until softened, 8 to 10 minutes.
3. Add fennel and garlic; cook and stir until fragrant, about 5 minutes.
4. Pour fish stock and vermouth into the pot.
5. Add tomatoes, red bell pepper, orange zest, red pepper flakes, pepper, and bay leaf.
6. Bring to a boil; reduce heat, cover, and simmer until red bell pepper is soft, about 20 minutes.
7. Bring stock back to a boil.
8. Add cod fillets; cook for 1 minute.
9. Add shrimp, clams, and mussels; cook, shaking pot occasionally, until shells open and shrimp turn opaque, 1 to 2 minutes.
