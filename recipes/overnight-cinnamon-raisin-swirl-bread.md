## Ingredients

- 2 (.25 ounce) packages active dry yeast
- ¾ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 5 cups all-purpose flour
- ½ cup white sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 4 teaspoons baking powder
- ½ cup shortening
- 2 cups lukewarm buttermilk
- 2 tablespoons butter, melted
- ¼ cup white sugar
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- ¼ cup raisins

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water.
2. Let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
3. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt and baking powder.
4. Cut in shortening until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
5. Add yeast mixture and buttermilk; mix well until dough pulls together.
6. Dough will be somewhat soft.
7. Place dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover, and refrigerate overnight.
8. The next morning, or when you are ready to bake your bread, take out your dough and knead in a little flour.
9. Let dough sit at room temperature for 45 minutes.
10. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
11. Press out dough onto a lightly floured surface to a rectangle.
12. Brush the surface with melted butter and sprinkle with the sugar, cinnamon and raisins.
13. Roll up dough and place, seam down, on a lightly greased baking sheet.
14. Bake in preheated oven for 25 to 35 minutes, or until bottom of loaf sounds hollow when tapped.
