## Ingredients

- 3 cups milk
- 1 cup butter
- ½ cup shortening
- 5 cups white sugar
- 3 (.25 ounce) packages active dry yeast
- ½ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
- 12  eggs, beaten
- 16 cups all-purpose flour
- 1  egg
- 2 tablespoons water

## Instructions

1. Warm the milk in a small saucepan until it bubbles.
2. Mix in the butter and shortening; stir until melted.
3. Place sugar in a very large bowl, pour milk over sugar and stir until sugar dissolves.
4. Let cool until lukewarm.
5. In a small bowl, stir yeast into warm water.
6. Stir 12 eggs and yeast mixture into milk and sugar; beat well.
7. Pour all of the flour into the milk/eggs/sugar mixture; stir well to combine.
8. When the dough has pulled together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic; about 8 minutes.
9. Lightly oil a large bowl, place the dough in the bowl and turn to coat with oil.
10. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place until doubled in volume, about 1 hour.
11. Deflate dough and let rise again until doubled, about 45 minutes.
12. Deflate the dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface.
13. Divide the dough into six equal pieces and form into loaves.
14. Place the loaves into lightly greased 9x5 inch loaf pans.
15. Cover the loaves with a damp cloth and let rise until doubled in volume, about 40 minutes.
16. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
17. In a small bowl, beat together 1 egg and 2 tablespoons water and brush on the risen loaves.
18. Bake in preheated oven for 25 to 30 minutes, or until bottom of loaves sound hollow when tapped.
