## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 ½ pounds cubed beef stew meat
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 20 small baby red potatoes
- 1 (14.5 ounce) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
- 3 large carrots, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 3 large stalks  celery, cut into 1-inch pieces
- ½ medium onion, roughly chopped
- 1 (1.5 ounce) package dry beef stew seasoning mix
- 1 tablespoon beef base (such as Better than Bouillon®)
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2  bay leaves
- 1 (16 ounce) carton chicken broth, or as needed

## Instructions

1. Turn on a multi-functional pressure cooker (such as Instant Pot®) and select Saute function.
2. Add oil and stew beef; season with salt and pepper.
3. Saute, stirring occasionally, until all beef is browned on all sides, 5 to 7 minutes.
4. Hit Cancel.
5. Add potatoes, fire-roasted tomatoes, carrots, celery, onion, seasoning mix, beef base, Worcestershire sauce, garlic, and bay leaves.
6. Pour in enough chicken broth to cover all ingredients without going over the 2/3-fill line.
7. Close and lock the lid.
8. Select high pressure according to manufacturer's instructions; set timer for 20 minutes.
9. Allow 10 to 15 minutes for pressure to build.
10. Release pressure using the natural-release method according to manufacturer's instructions, for 10 minutes.
11. Then release remaining pressure carefully using the quick-release method according to manufacturer's instructions, about 5 minutes.
12. Unlock and remove the lid.
