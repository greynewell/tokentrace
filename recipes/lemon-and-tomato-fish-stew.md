## Ingredients

- 1 cup brown rice
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- ½  onion, chopped
- 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1  Hatch chile pepper, seeded and chopped
- 1  Roma tomato, chopped
- 1 ½ tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 cup chicken broth
- ½ cup white wine
- 2  bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 2  salmon fillets
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- ¼ cup sour cream
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice, or to taste
- 1 bunch green onions, chopped

## Instructions

1. Bring water and brown rice to a boil in a saucepan.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and liquid has been absorbed, 45 to 50 minutes.
3. Heat oil in a deep skillet over high heat.
4. Add onion, celery, and garlic; cook and stir until edges of the onion are browned, about 5 minutes.
5. Mix in chile pepper, tomato, and tomato paste; cook, stirring constantly, until softened, about 1 minute.
6. Stir chicken broth, white wine, and bay leaves into the skillet; simmer until stew is evenly warmed, about 5 minutes.
7. Melt butter in a pot over medium heat.
8. Add salmon; season with salt and pepper.
9. Cook, flipping once, until salmon flakes easily with a fork, 3 to 5 minutes per side.
10. Separate into small pieces.
11. Stir sour cream and lemon juice into the stew; pour stew over salmon.
12. Remove bay leaves.
13. Add green onions.
14. Divide brown rice evenly between 4 bowls; ladle stew over rice.
