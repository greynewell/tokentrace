## Ingredients

- ½ cup milk
- 2  hard rolls, day-old
- ½ pound ground beef
- ½ pound ground pork
- 2  eggs
- 1  onion, minced
- 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
- 1 teaspoon dried marjoram
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons butter

## Instructions

1. Pour the milk into a bowl; soak the rolls in the milk for about 15 minutes.
2. Place the milk-soaked rolls, beef, pork, eggs, onion, parsley, marjoram, garlic, salt, and pepper in a large bowl; mix with your hands until evenly combined.
3. Divide the mixture into eight portions and roll them into balls.
4. Flatten the balls to make 1-inch thick patties.
5. Melt the butter in a large skillet.
6. Fry each patty in the melted butter until meat has reached an internal temperature of 160 degrees F (72 degrees C), about 6 minutes each side.
7. These can be served either hot or cold.
