## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 1  yellow onion, diced
- 1  red bell pepper, diced
- 1  jalapeno pepper, diced
- 1 pound ground venison
- 1 pinch ground black pepper, or to taste
- 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper, or to taste
- 1 pinch grill seasoning (such as Montreal Steak Seasoning®), or to taste
- 1 pinch garlic salt, or to taste
- 1 dash Worcestershire sauce
- 1  vine-ripened tomato, diced
- 2 tablespoons barbeque sauce, or more to taste
- 2 tablespoons ketchup (such as Heinz®), or more to taste
- 2 teaspoons tomato paste, or more to taste
- 2 teaspoons hot sauce, or more to taste
- 4  hamburger buns, split
- 1 (8 ounce) package shredded sharp Cheddar cheese

## Instructions

1. Heat 1 1/2 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
2. Add onion, red bell pepper, and jalapeno; cook and stir until softened, 5 to 8 minutes.
3. Heat remaining 1 1/2 tablespoon oil in another skillet over medium heat.
4. Add venison; cook and stir until lightly browned, 4 to 5 minutes.
5. Stir in black pepper, cayenne pepper, grill seasoning, garlic salt, and Worcestershire sauce; cook until venison is browned, 4 to 5 minutes.
6. Combine tomato, barbeque sauce, ketchup, tomato paste, and hot sauce in a food processor; pulse until sauce is combined.
7. Combine onion mixture, venison mixture, and sauce in a saucepan.
8. Simmer until flavors combine, 10 to 15 minutes.
9. Divide among buns; top with Cheddar cheese.
