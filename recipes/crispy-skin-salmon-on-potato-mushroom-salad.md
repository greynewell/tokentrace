## Ingredients

- ½ pound Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled
- 2 (4 ounce) skin-on, boneless salmon fillets
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 9 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 1  lemon, juiced, divided
- 1 teaspoon herb mustard (such as tarragon mustard)
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 2 cups mushrooms, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 clove garlic, peeled
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Place potatoes into a large pot and cover with salted water; bring to a boil.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, 10 to 20 minutes.
3. Drain and cut into bite-size chunks.
4. Cut 1/2-inch deep slashes lengthwise down the skin-side of each fillet.
5. Season each fillet with salt and black pepper and rub 1/2 teaspoon olive oil into each.
6. Stir tomatoes, 3 tablespoons olive oil, 1/2 of the lemon juice, herb mustard, salt, and black pepper together in a bowl until well-combined.
7. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate until chilled.
8. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
9. Cook and stir mushrooms and whole garlic clove over medium heat until mushrooms start to wilt, 5 to 10 minutes; remove garlic from skillet.
10. Stir potato chunks, salt, and cayenne pepper into mushrooms; squeeze remaining lemon juice into skillet.
11. Cook and stir until potatoes are crispy and golden, 10 to 12 minutes.
12. Heat remaining olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium-high heat.
13. Place salmon, skin-side down, in hot skillet and cook until fish is white about halfway up the sides, 5 to 6 minutes.
14. Flip fish and cook until desired doneness is reached, 3 to 5 minutes more.
15. Divide potato-mushroom mixture between 2 plates and top each with a salmon fillet.
16. Garnish with tomato mixture.
