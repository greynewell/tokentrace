## Ingredients

- ⅓ cup semisweet chocolate chips
- 1 ½ cups vanilla wafer crumbs
- ¾ cup hazelnuts - toasted, skinned and coarsely chopped
- 2 tablespoons white sugar
- 3 tablespoons butter, melted
- 3 (8 ounce) packages cream cheese, softened
- 1 cup white sugar
- 3  eggs, lightly beaten
- 3 tablespoons hazelnut liqueur
- 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
- ⅔ cup semisweet chocolate chips
- 13  skinned, toasted hazelnuts
- ¼ cup sour cream, room temperature
- 1 tablespoon hazelnut liqueur

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C).
2. Using a blender or a food processor, finely chop 1/3 cup semisweet chocolate chips.
3. Transfer ground chocolate to a mixing bowl.
4. Add vanilla wafer crumbs, ground hazelnuts, 2 tablespoons white sugar, and melted butter or margarine.
5. Mix until well combined.
6. Press onto the bottom and up the sides of a 9 inch springform pan.
7. Bake in preheated oven for 15 minutes.
8. Allow to cool.
9. Raise oven temperature to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
10. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until fluffy.
11. Gradually add 1 cup white sugar; mix well.
12. Add the eggs and 3 tablespoons liqueur.
13. Mix until well blended.
14. Coarsely chop 1 cup of the semisweet chocolate chips, and stir the chocolate into the cream cheese mixture.
15. Pour batter into the cooled crust.
16. Bake in preheated 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) oven for 1 hour.
17. Let cake cool in oven for 1 hour.
18. Remove outer ring from pan; allow cake to cool completely.
19. Melt 2/3 cup semisweet chocolate chips over hot (not boiling) water.
20. Stir until smooth.
21. Dip 13 hazelnuts into the chocolate, covering one-half of each nut.
22. Shake off the excess chocolate.
23. Place on a waxed-paper lined plate and chill until set.
24. Stir the sour cream into the remaining melted chocolate and mix well.
25. Add 1 tablespoon liqueur.
26. Spread glaze on top of the cooled cheesecake.
27. Garnish with chocolate-dipped hazelnuts.
