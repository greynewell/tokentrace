## Ingredients

- cooking spray
- 4 cups water
- ¼ cup baking soda
- 1 (11 ounce) package refrigerated French bread dough (such as Pillsbury®)
- 1  egg yolk
- 1 tablespoon water
- 1 tablespoon coarse sea salt
- 2 tablespoons butter, melted

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
2. Grease a baking sheet with cooking spray.
3. Mix 4 cups water and baking soda together in a large saucepan; bring to a boil.
4. Pour into a shallow dish.
5. Flatten bread dough on a floured work surface; cut into 3 pieces, each about 6 inches long.
6. Slice each piece into 4 long strips.
7. Break a 1-inch piece off each strip; roll into a ball for the tail.
8. Roll the remainder of each strip into a log about 9 inches long.
9. Wrap 1 log around the tail to make the body; twist twice to make the head and ears.
10. Repeat with remaining 11 strips.
11. Submerge "bunnies," 2 at a time, into the water-baking soda mixture for 30 seconds.
12. Transfer to the prepared baking sheet.
13. Adjust ears if they became untwisted.
14. Whisk egg yolk and 1 tablespoon water together in a small bowl.
15. Brush over bunnies.
16. Sprinkle salt on top.
17. Bake in the preheated oven until warmed through, about 5 minutes.
18. Move around with a spatula to ensure they don't stick.
19. Continue baking until golden brown, about 10 minutes.
20. Transfer to cooling racks.
21. Brush with melted butter while still warm.
