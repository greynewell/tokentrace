## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- ¾ teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
- ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
- ¼ teaspoon hot chili powder
- ¼ teaspoon ground paprika
- ¼ cup salted butter
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 1 small bell pepper, chopped
- 1 stalk celery, chopped
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour, divided
- 2 cups chicken stock
- 4 ounces tomato paste
- 1 (16 ounce) can tomato sauce
- 1 ½ pounds Okra, raw
- 2 (16 ounce) cans whole kernel corn, drained
- 3 pounds medium raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- ¼ cup seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®)
- ½ cup cold water

## Instructions

1. Prepare seasoning mixture: mix salt, garlic powder, black pepper, cayenne, chili powder, and paprika together in a bowl.
2. Set aside.
3. Prepare gumbo: melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat.
4. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery; saute until onion is translucent, 5 to 7 minutes.
5. Add seasoning mixture and stir until vegetables are well coated.
6. Stir in 1 tablespoon flour and mix until combined, being careful not to burn.
7. Slowly add chicken stock.
8. Transfer mixture to a Dutch oven or large stockpot.
9. Cover and cook over medium heat until reduced slightly, about 30 minutes.
10. Add tomato paste and stir until dissolved.
11. Add tomato sauce, cover, and cook until reduced slightly, about 15 minutes.
12. Add okra and corn.
13. Cook, stirring occasionally so veggies don't stick to the bottom of the pot, until okra is tender, 5 to 10 minutes.
14. Season shrimp with seafood seasoning.
15. Add to the pot.
16. Cover and cook until shrimp are bright pink on the outside and the meat is opaque, about 5 minutes.
17. Taste and adjust seasoning, if desired.
18. Combine water and remaining 1 tablespoon flour to make a roux.
19. Add to gumbo as needed and cook until thickened to desired consistency.
