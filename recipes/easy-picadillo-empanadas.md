## Ingredients

- 2 (7.5 ounce) packages refrigerated buttermilk biscuits (not the layered varieties)
- Cornmeal for rolling
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- ½  medium Granny Smith apple, cut into small dice
- ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
- ⅛ teaspoon ground cloves
- 2 cups shredded meat from a rotisserie chicken
- 1 (16 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
- ¼ cup seedless raisins
- ¼ cup chopped pimento-stuffed olives
- 2 large garlic cloves, minced
- ¼ cup toasted slivered almonds
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Olive oil, for brushing

## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to center; heat oven to 450 degrees.
2. Heat oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Saute onion and apple until golden, 3 to 4 minutes.
4. Add spices; saute until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
5. Stir in chicken, tomatoes, raisins and olives; simmer until filling is thick and moist but not too liquid, 5 to 7 minutes longer.
6. Stir in garlic, almonds, salt and pepper.
7. Transfer to a medium bowl; cool to room temperature.
8. Separate biscuits onto a cornmeal-coated work surface.
9. Sprinkle with cornmeal as necessary, and roll each one into a 5 inch circle.
10. Fill each one, using a scant 2 Tbs. filling and leaving a 1/2-inch border.
11. Place on cookie sheets lined with parchment paper. (At this point, can be frozen up to 2 weeks.) Brush tops with oil.
12. Bake until golden, 16 to 20 minutes (longer if frozen).
