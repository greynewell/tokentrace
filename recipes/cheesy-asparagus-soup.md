## Ingredients

- 6 cups chicken stock, divided
- 1 ½ pounds asparagus, trimmed and chopped
- ¼ cup butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1  onion, chopped
- 2 ribs celery, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1 cup half-and-half, or more to taste
- 1 pound processed American cheese (such as Velveeta®), cubed
- salt and ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Bring 4 cups chicken stock to a boil in a saucepan.
2. Add asparagus and cook until tender, 3 to 5 minutes.
3. Drain asparagus, reserving the stock.
4. Set a few asparagus tips aside for garnish.
5. Heat butter and olive oil in a stockpot over medium heat.
6. Add onion and celery and cook until tender, 6 to 8 minutes.
7. Add garlic; cook and stir until fragrant, about 3 minutes more.
8. Stir flour into onion mixture until combined, about 2 minutes.
9. Add remaining 2 cups chicken stock; cook and stir until thickened, about 5 minutes more.
10. Add cooked asparagus stalks and remaining reserved asparagus-chicken stock; bring soup to a boil.
11. Heat half-and-half in a small saucepan over medium heat until warmed slightly, 1 to 2 minutes.
12. Add warmed half-and-half to soup, reduce heat, and simmer until flavors have blended, about 10 minutes.
13. Pour soup into a blender no more than half full.
14. Cover and hold lid down; pulse a few times before leaving on to blend.
15. Puree in batches until smooth.
16. Return pureed soup to stockpot.
17. Add American cheese and stir until melted; season with salt and pepper.
18. Garnish with asparagus tips.
