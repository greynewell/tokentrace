## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided, or as needed
- 1 pound fingerling potatoes
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder, divided
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 (6 ounce) skin-on salmon fillets
- 2 teaspoons seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®), or to taste
- ¼ cup butter, at room temperature
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh sage
- 1 tablespoon olive oil, or to taste
- ½  lemon, juiced, or to taste
- 1 package microgreens

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C). (If using a countertop induction oven, use "Auto-Cook" option 2.
2. Choose setting and enter fish weight.) Pour 1 tablespoon olive oil over fingerling potatoes in the bowl.
3. Season with 1 teaspoon garlic powder, salt, and pepper; toss to combine.
4. Rub 1 1/2 teaspoon olive oil over each salmon fillet.
5. Season each with 1 teaspoon seafood seasoning.
6. Arrange salmon, skin-side up, in center of a cast iron skillet or baking dish. (Place salmon on the grill pan if using countertop induction oven.) Bake in the preheated oven until bottom of salmon is lightly browned, 4 to 5 minutes.
7. Flip salmon and continue cooking until fish flakes easily with a fork, 10 to 15 minutes. (For countertop induction oven, cook on medium-high grill setting for 1 1/2 minutes with skin side up.
8. Flip and grill salmon until fish flakes easily with a fork, 2 1/2 to 3 minutes more.) Heat cast iron skillet over medium-high heat; cook and stir potatoes until browned and cooked through, 15 to 20 minutes.
9. Combine remaining 1 teaspoon garlic powder, butter, thyme, and sage in a small microwave-safe bowl.
10. Heat in the microwave until butter is melted, 20 to 30 seconds.
11. Mix well.
12. Pour over cooked potatoes.
13. Whisk 1 tablespoon olive oil and lemon juice together in a bowl until dressing is well mixed.
14. Toss microgreens and dressing together in a bowl.
15. Place salmon on serving plates; garnish with micro greens.
16. Serve potatoes alongside.
