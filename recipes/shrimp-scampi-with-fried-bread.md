## Ingredients

- 6 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 4 tablespoons minced garlic, divided
- 3 tablespoons shredded Parmesan cheese, divided
- ¾ stick butter, divided
- 2 tablespoons minced shallots
- 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper
- 1 ½ pounds large, head-on shrimp
- ½ cup dry white wine
- 1  lemon, juiced
- 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning  (Optional)
- 1  baguette, cut into 4-inch slices
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley, or as desired

## Instructions

1. Combine 3 tablespoons oil, 1 tablespoon garlic, and 1 tablespoon Parmesan cheese in a bowl.
2. Stir and let sit for 30 minutes.
3. Melt 3 tablespoons butter in a pan over medium heat.
4. Whisk in remaining 3 tablespoons oil.
5. Add shallots and red pepper and cook until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
6. Reduce heat to medium-low.
7. Add remaining garlic and shrimp.
8. Cook, tossing frequently in butter, until cooked through and pink, about 5 minutes.
9. Press down on the heads every so often to press juices into the sauce.
10. Transfer shrimp to a bowl, leaving pan on the stove.
11. Add white wine to the pan over medium heat and scrape up any cooked bits from the bottom.
12. Let liquid reduce by 1/2, about 5 minutes.
13. While sauce reduces, peel and de-vein shrimp.
14. Add lemon juice, 4 tablespoons butter, and 2 tablespoons Parmesan cheese to the pan.
15. Add Cajun seasoning.
16. Cook, whisking frequently, until sauce thickens, about 15 minutes.
17. Meanwhile, heat 4 tablespoons butter in a separate pan over medium heat.
18. Brush baguette slices on both sides with reserved oil-Parmesan cheese mixture.
19. Fry the bread in the hot butter until golden brown on each side, about 5 minutes.
20. Transfer bread to a paper towel-lined plate.
21. Add peeled shrimp to the sauce.
22. Stir and simmer 2 to 3 minutes.
23. Season with salt and pepper.
24. Plate shrimp over fried baguettes.
25. Spoon extra sauce on top and garnish with parsley.
26. Serve immediately.
