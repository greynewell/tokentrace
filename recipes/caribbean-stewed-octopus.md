## Ingredients

- ¼ cup butter, divided
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 small yellow onion, diced
- 1 (1 pound) piece octopus, beak and eyes removed, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 cup crushed ice
- 2 tablespoons minced fresh ginger root
- 2 large cloves garlic, minced
- ½ (750 milliliter) bottle dry white wine (such as Pinot Grigio)

## Instructions

1. Heat 1 tablespoon butter and oil in a skillet over medium heat; stir in onion.
2. Cook and stir until onion has softened and turned translucent, about 5 minutes.
3. Reduce heat to low.
4. Continue cooking, stirring frequently, until onion is very tender and dark brown, about 30 minutes more.
5. Rinse octopus.
6. Place on a flat work surface and cut tentacles into thirds.
7. Chop the head into bite-sized pieces.
8. Place octopus pieces in a gallon-sized resealable plastic bag.
9. Mix in salt and add crushed ice.
10. Seal the bag and place into a larger grocery bag.
11. Pound the octopus with the smooth side of a meat mallet until as tender as possible.
12. Remove octopus from the bag and let drain in a colander.
13. Stir ginger and garlic into the skillet with the onion; cook until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes.
14. Pour in wine and the remaining 3 tablespoons butter.
15. Increase heat and bring to a boil.
16. Add octopus; reduce heat to a simmer.
17. Cover and cook, stirring every 10 minutes, until flavors meld, about 1 hour.
18. Uncover and continue simmering until sauce is thickened to your liking and octopus is tender, about 1 hour more.
