## Ingredients

- 2 (5 ounce) salmon fillets
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon dried dill
- 4 tablespoons light brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons teriyaki sauce
- 2 tablespoons honey
- ¼ cup dry white wine

## Instructions

1. Wash salmon fillets under cold water and pat dry with a paper towel.
2. Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and dill.
3. Combine brown sugar, olive oil, soy sauce, teriyaki sauce, and honey in a mixing bowl.
4. Stir until brown sugar is dissolved.
5. Pour into a large glass or ceramic bowl.
6. Add salmon, skin-side up.
7. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and marinate in the refrigerator for 1 hour.
8. Set an oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler to high.
9. Remove salmon from the marinade, shake off excess, and place on a foil-lined baking sheet, skin-side down.
10. Pour marinade into a small pan and add wine.
11. Bring marinade-wine mixture to a boil.
12. Reduce heat and simmer until reduced by half, about 3 minutes.
13. Cook salmon under the preheated broiler for 5 minutes.
14. Spoon some of the reduced sauce over the salmon and continue to cook until fish flakes easily with a fork, about 5 more minutes.
15. Serve immediately.
