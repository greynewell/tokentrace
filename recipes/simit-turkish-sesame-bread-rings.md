## Ingredients

- ½ cup sesame seeds
- 4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 5 tablespoons water
- 5 tablespoons milk
- ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
- ¼ cup olive oil
- 1  egg, lightly beaten

## Instructions

1. Toast sesame seeds in a dry frying pan over low heat until golden and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
2. Keep a close eye as they change color very quickly.
3. Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl.
4. Make a well in the flour mixture and add water, milk, melted butter, and olive oil; stir well until dough pulls together.
5. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 8 minutes.
6. Place dough in a large, lightly-oiled bowl and turn to coat.
7. Cover with a towel and let rise in a warm place until doubled in volume, 30 minutes to 1 hour.
8. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
9. Divide dough in to 8 parts and roll each part into a 6- to 8-inch long rope.
10. Join ends together and seal to form a ring.
11. Place rings on prepared baking sheet, cover with a clean kitchen towel and let rise for 15 minutes.
12. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
13. Brush rings with beaten egg and sprinkle generously with toasted sesame seeds.
14. Bake in the preheated oven until golden and crusty, about 20 minutes.
