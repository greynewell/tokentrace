## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon minced fresh garlic
- 1 tablespoon chopped onion
- ½  red bell pepper, diced
- 1 cup papaya - peeled, seeded and cubed
- 1  habanero pepper, seeded and chopped (wear gloves)
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch
- 1 tablespoon water
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 1 tablespoon melted butter
- 3 (4 ounce) fillets mahi mahi
- 3 tablespoons sliced almonds
- 1 pinch salt

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven's broiler and set the oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source.
2. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
3. Stir in the garlic and onion; cook and stir until the onion begins to soften, about 2 minutes.
4. Add the red bell pepper, papaya, and habanero pepper.
5. Continue cooking a few more minutes until the bell pepper begins to soften.
6. Stir together the cornstarch, water, and 2 tablespoons of lime juice.
7. Stir into the papaya sauce until thickened, stirring constantly.
8. Keep the sauce warm over very low heat.
9. Stir 1 tablespoon of lime juice together with the melted butter, and brush on the mahi mahi fillets.
10. Place onto a  broiler pan.
11. Cook under the preheated broiler for 4 minutes, then turn the fish over, and continue cooking for 4 minutes more.
12. Sprinkle the almonds over the fish and return to the broiler until the almonds are crisp and the fish flakes easily with a fork, about 2 minutes.
13. Season to taste with salt and serve with the papaya sauce.
