## Ingredients

- 2 cups shrimp - deveined, cut into strips, heads reserved
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- ⅓ cup diced ham
- 1  chorizo de bilbao (spicy Spanish semi-cured sausage), cut into strips
- 1 (18.4 ounce) lean pork tenderloin, diced
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon white sugar
- ½ head cabbage, shredded
- 1  potato, cut into strips
- ⅔ cup chickpeas, drained
- 2 stalks celery, cut crosswise into slices
- 1  carrot, cut into strips
- 1  ubod (heart of palm), cut into strips
- 25 leaves lettuce
- 25  lumpia wrappers

## Instructions

1. Pound shrimp heads in a mortar until about 1/2 cup juice is released.
2. Drain juice and reserve.
3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
4. Add garlic; cook and stir until slightly brown, 1 to 2 minutes.
5. Add onion; cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
6. Stir in ham and chorizo; cook until juices release, about 2 minutes.
7. Add pork; cook and stir until no longer pink, about 7 minutes.
8. Season with salt and pepper.
9. Place shrimp into the skillet; cook until opaque, about 3 minutes.
10. Add the reserved shrimp juice and mix thoroughly.
11. Add soy sauce and sugar; cook and stir until combined, about 2 minutes more.
12. Add cabbage, potato, chickpeas, celery, carrot, and ubod.
13. Cook until vegetables are tender, about 3 minutes.
14. Let filling cool until safe to handle, at least 10 minutes.
15. Lay a lettuce leaf on a lumpia wrapper.
16. Place about 2 tablespoons of the filling over the center of the lettuce.
17. Fold 1 end of the wrapper over the filling to secure the lower end; roll wrapper over to cover.
18. Repeat with remaining lettuce, wrappers, and filling.
